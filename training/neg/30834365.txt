Complete Genome Sequence of the Tataguine Virus, Isolated in the Central African 
Republic in 1972 from a Human with an Acute Febrile Syndrome.

Simo Tchetgna HD(1), Selekon B(2), Kazanji M(2), Berthet N(1)(3)(4), Nakoune
E(2).

Author information:

Tataguine virus (TATV) is an orthobunyavirus that causes febrile illnesses in
Africa. Here, we report the complete genome sequences of TATV strain HB72P583,
isolated in the Central African Republic in 1972. Several genetic variations were
detected in the small (S), medium (M), and large (L) segments relative to a TATV 
strain isolated in Nigeria in 1966.
