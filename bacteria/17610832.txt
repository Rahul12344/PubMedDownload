Ecology: death and destruction determine diversity.

Brockhurst MA(1).

Author information: 
(1)School of Biological Sciences, Biosciences Building, University of Liverpool, 
Liverpool, UK. michael.brockhurst@liverpool.ac.uk

Death can be good, bad or indifferent for biodiversity. New work confirms that
predators can drive diversification of prey even in homogeneous environments, but
suggests that this effect is crucially dependent upon the frequency and intensity
of other mass-mortality events.
