Enterobius vermicularis: 10,000-year-old human infection.

Fry GF, Moore JG.

Eggs of Enterobius vermicularis (human pinworm) were found in hum coprolites from
Hopug and Danger Caves, western Utah. The Caves were inhabitated by man from
10,000 B.C. to A.D. 1400. The oldest coprolite containing dated at 7837 B.C. This
represents the earliest known association between man abd this exclusively human 
parasite.
