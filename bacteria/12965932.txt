Lipoteichoic acid and peptidoglycan from Staphylococcus aureus synergistically
induce neutrophil influx into the lungs of mice.

Leemans JC(1), Heikens M, van Kessel KP, Florquin S, van der Poll T.

Author information: 
(1)Laboratory of Experimental Internal Medicine, Utrecht University, Utrecht, The
Netherlands. j.c.leemans@amc.uva.nl

Staphylococcus aureus is an important pathogen in nosocomial pneumonia.
Lipoteichoic acid (LTA) and peptidoglycan (PepG) are part of the staphylococcal
cell wall. Here we show that LTA and PepG act in synergy to cause
polymorphonuclear cell recruitment in the pulmonary compartment during S. aureus 
pneumonia.
