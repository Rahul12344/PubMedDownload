Real-Time Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR) for the Analysis of Interactions
Between SUMO Traps and Mono- or PolySUMO Moieties.

Xolalpa W(1), Rodriguez MS(2), England P(3)(4).

Author information: 
(1)Centro de Investigación sobre Enfermedades Infecciosas, Instituto Nacional de 
Salud Pública, 62100, Cuernavaca, Morelos, Mexico.
(2)INBIOMED, San Sebastian, Spain.
(3)Plate-forme de Biophysique des Macromolécules et de leurs Interactions,

(4)CNRS-UMR 3528, 75724, Paris, France. Patrick.england@pasteur.fr.

Isolating endogenous SUMOylated proteins is a challenging task due to the high
reversibility of this posttranslational modification. We have shown that SUMO
traps are useful tools for the enrichment and isolation of proteins modified by
SUMO in vitro and in vivo. To characterize the affinity and specificity of
different SUMO chains for these traps, that are based on SUMO-interacting motifs,
we have used real-time surface plasmon resonance (SPR), which allows a label-free
analysis of protein/protein interactions. Here, a protocol to determine the
affinities of multivalent SUMO traps for polySUMO chains or mono-SUMO molecules
by SPR is presented.
