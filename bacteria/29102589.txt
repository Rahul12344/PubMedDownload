Gonococcal Arthritis and C2 Deficiency.

[Article in English, Spanish]

Benavent Núñez D(1), Tornero Marín C(2), Bonilla Hernán G(2), García Perea A(3),

Disseminated gonococcal infection is a rare presentation of the sexually
transmitted pathogen, Neisseria gonorrhoeae. Here, we report the case of a
64-year-old woman with disseminated gonococcal infection, which started with
symptoms of oligoarthritis and malaise. Neisseria gonorrhoeae was identified in
the carpal synovial fluid. The follow-up study revealed an absence of total
hemolytic complement and complement C2 was not detected. Being relatively common,
C2 deficiency has been associated with disseminated gonococcal infection in a few
cases. We present a new case and discuss those previously published.
