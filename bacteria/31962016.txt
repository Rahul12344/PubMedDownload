A Mycoplasma Genomic DNA Probe using Gated Nanoporous Anodic Alumina.

A nanoporous anodic alumina (NAA)-based sensor system for the detection of
Mycoplasma was developed through the implementation of "molecular gates"
selective to the presence of this bacterium. The capped support showed a
negligible cargo release, while presence of Mycoplasma genomic DNA resulted in
the release of rhodamine B fluorescent dye. This sensor system presents a limit
of detection of 20 genomic DNA copies⋅μL-1 and was applied to the detection of
Mycoplasma bacteria in competitive environments, such as culture cell media.
