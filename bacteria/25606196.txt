Drug reaction with herbal supplement: a possible case of drug induced lupus
erythematosus.

Leelavathi M(1), Jamani N(1), Muhammad M(2), Adawiyah J(3), Aziz N(1).

Author information: 
(1)Department of Family Medicine, Faculty of Medicine, Universiti Kebangsaan
Malaysia (Leelavathi Muthupalaniappen, Nurjasmine Aida Jamani, Noor Azah Abdul
Aziz).
(2)Department of Microbiology and Medical Immunology, Faculty of Medicine,
Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (Marlyn Muhammad).
(3)Dermatology Unit, Faculty of Medicine, Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia
(Adawiyah Jamil).

A 24-year-old lady presented with four days history of fever, non-pruritic rash, 
ankle pain and swelling. She had consumed herbal supplement five days before the 
onset of symptoms. Examinations revealed erythematous maculo-papular lesions of
varying sizes on sun exposed areas. Patient was suspected to have Drug Induced
Lupus Erythematosus (DILE) and subsequently symptoms subsided rapidly on
withholding the herbal medication.
