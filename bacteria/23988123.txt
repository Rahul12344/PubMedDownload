Structural requirements of 2-oxoglutaric acid analogues to mimic its signaling
function.

Liu X(1), Wang Y, Laurini E, Posocco P, Chen H, Ziarelli F, Janicki A, Qu F,

A 2-oxoglutaric acid (2-OG) probe bearing a methylene group introduced at the C4 
position and a vinyl group to replace the carbonyl group at the C2 position
elicited characteristic affinity for NtcA, the 2-OG receptor, while maintaining
the signaling function of the parent natural metabolite 2-OG. This discovery
opens new perspectives in the design, synthesis, and implementation of specific
2-OG analogues as molecular probes for investigating the complex 2-OG signaling
pathways.
