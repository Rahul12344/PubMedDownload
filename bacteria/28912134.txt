Comment in
    Cell Res. 2017 Dec;27(12):1405-1406.

Topoisomerase 2 (TOP2) DNA transactions proceed via formation of the TOP2
cleavage complex (TOP2cc), a covalent enzyme-DNA reaction intermediate that is
vulnerable to trapping by potent anticancer TOP2 drugs. How genotoxic TOP2
DNA-protein cross-links are resolved is unclear. We found that the SUMO (small
ubiquitin-related modifier) ligase ZATT (ZNF451) is a multifunctional DNA repair 
factor that controls cellular responses to TOP2 damage. ZATT binding to TOP2cc
facilitates a proteasome-independent tyrosyl-DNA phosphodiesterase 2 (TDP2)
hydrolase activity on stalled TOP2cc. The ZATT SUMO ligase activity further
promotes TDP2 interactions with SUMOylated TOP2, regulating efficient TDP2
recruitment through a "split-SIM" SUMO2 engagement platform. These findings
uncover a ZATT-TDP2-catalyzed and SUMO2-modulated pathway for direct resolution
of TOP2cc.
