Comment on
    Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A. 2016 Apr 12;113(15):E2114-23.

Hall et al. have strategically used long-read sequencing technology to
characterize the structure and highly repetitive content of the Y chromosome in
Anopheles malaria mosquitoes. Their work confirms that this important but elusive
heterochromatic sex chromosome is evolving extremely rapidly and harbors a
remarkably small number of genes.
