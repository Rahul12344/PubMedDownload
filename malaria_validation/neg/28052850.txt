Association between Polymorphisms in the Pfmdr6 Gene and Ex Vivo Susceptibility
to Quinine in Plasmodium falciparum Parasites from Dakar, Senegal.

Polymorphisms and the overexpression of transporter genes, especially of the
ATP-binding cassette superfamily, have been involved in antimalarial drug
resistance. The objective of this study was to use 77 Senegalese Plasmodium
falciparum isolates to evaluate the association between the number of Asn
residues in the polymorphic microsatellite region of the Plasmodium falciparum
multidrug resistance 6 gene (Pfmdr6) and the ex vivo susceptibility to
antimalarials. A significant association was observed between the presence of 7
or 9 Asn repeats and reduced susceptibility to quinine.
