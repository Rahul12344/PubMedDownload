background: different proteins derived membrane apical organelles
become involved malarial parasite invasion host cells. among these, the
rhoptry neck proteins (rons) interact protein component micronemes 
to enable formation strong bond crucial parasite's
successful invasion. present study aimed identifying and
characterizing ron5 protein plasmodium vivax evaluating ability to
bind reticulocytes.
methods: taking plasmodium falciparum plasmodium knowlesi ron5 amino acid
sequences template, in-silico search made p. vivax genome for
identifying orthologous gene. different molecular tools used for
experimentally ascertaining pvron5 gene presence transcription p. vivax
vcg-1 strain schizonts. polyclonal antibodies pvron5 peptides used
for evaluating protein expression (by western blot) sub-cellular localization
(by immunofluorescence). 33 kda pvron5 fragment expressed escherichia
coli used evaluating reactivity sera patients infected p. 
vivax. two assays made determining ron5 recombinant fragment's
ability bind reticulocyte-enriched human umbilical cord samples.
results: pvron5 gene (3,477 bp) transcribed vcg-1 strain schizonts and
encoded ~133 kda protein expressed rhoptry neck vcg-1
strain late schizonts, together pvron2 pvron4. polyclonal sera against
pvron5 peptides specifically detected ~85 ~30 kda fragments parasite
lysate, thereby suggesting proteolytic processing protein. comparative
analysis vcg-1 strain pvron5 p. vivax strains different
geographic localizations suggested low polymorphism regarding malarial 
antigens. recombinant fragment pvron5 protein (rpvron5) recognized
by sera p. vivax-infected patients bound red blood cells, a
marked preference human reticulocytes.
conclusions: pvron5 gene transcribed vcg-1 strain, encoded
protein expressed parasite's apical pole might participating 
merozoite invasion host cells, taking account marked binding
preference human reticulocytes.
