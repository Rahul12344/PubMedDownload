objective: analyze transcription pattern neuropeptides ontogeny 
of malaria vector, mosquito anopheles albimanus.
materials methods: transcription pattern crustacean cardioactive
peptide (ccap), corazonin, ecdysis triggering hormone (eth), allatostatin-a,
orcokinin, insulin like peptide 2 (ilp2), insulin like peptide 5 (ilp5) and
bursicon evaluated using qpcr larvae (1st - 4th instar), pupae adult
mosquitoes.
results: unlike insects, transcripts ccap (70.8%), eth (60.2%) and
corazonin (76.5%) expressed 4th instar larvae, probably these
three neuropeptides associated beginning ecdysis. the
neuropeptide ilp2 showed higher transcription levels stages and
orcokinin decreased development mosquito.
conclusions: ccap, corazonin eth neuropeptidesare potential targets for
the design control strategies aimed disrupting an. albiamnus larval
development.
