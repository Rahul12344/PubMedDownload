the archipelago são tomé principe (stp), west africa, suffered the
heavy burden malaria since 16th century. last decade, after
a successful control program stp become low transmission country one of
the nations decreases 90% malaria admission death
rates. carried longitudinal study determine genetic structure 
stp parasite populations time space. twelve microsatellite loci were
genotyped plasmodium falciparum samples two islands collected 1997,
2000 2004. analysis performed proportions mixed genotype
infections, allelic diversity, population differentiation, effective population
size bottleneck effects. found high levels genetic diversity and
minimal inter-population genetic differentiation typical african continental
regions intense stable malaria transmission. detected significant
differences years, special emphasis 1997 showed the
highest proportion samples infected p. falciparum highest mean
number haplotypes per isolate. study establishes comprehensive genetic 
data baseline pre-intervention scenario future studies; taking into
account recent successful control intervention territory.
