background: artemisinin-based combination therapy (act) introduced the
mid-1990s recommended since 2005 world health organization as
first-line treatment plasmodium falciparum endemic countries. in
2010, combination dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (dp) recommended the
treatment uncomplicated p. falciparum malaria. dp one first-line
treatments used french army since 2013.
case presentation: case p. falciparum clinical failure dp day 20 was
described 104 kg french soldier deployed djibouti. admitted to
hospital supervision oral treatment dp [40 mg dihydroartemisinin
(dha) plus 320 mg piperaquine tetraphosphate (ppq)]. corresponded a
cumulative dose 4.6 mg/kg dha 37 mg/kg ppq present patient, which
is far recommended ranges. mutation found propeller
domain kelch 13 (k13) gene, associated artemisinin
resistance southeast asia. pfmdr1 n86, 184f, s1034 n1042 polymorphisms and
haplotype 72-76 cviet pfcrt gene found present case. there
was evidence resistance dp.
conclusion: case confirms risk therapeutic failure with
dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine under-dosing patients weighing than
100 kg. therapeutic failure dp under-dosing highlighted the
importance appropriate dosing guidelines need research data
(efficacy, pharmacokinetics pharmacodynamics) over-weight patient group.
