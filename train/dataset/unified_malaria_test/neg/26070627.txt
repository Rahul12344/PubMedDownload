background: human malaria parasite plasmodium falciparum complex and
multi-stage life cycle requires extensive precise gene regulation to
allow invasion hijacking host cells, transmission, immune escape. to
date, regulatory elements orchestrating critical parasite processes
remain largely unknown. yet becoming increasingly clear long
non-coding rnas (lncrnas) could represent missing regulatory layer across a
broad range organisms.
results: investigate regulatory capacity lncrna p. falciparum, we
harvested fifteen samples two time-courses. sample set profiled 56 h 
p. falciparum blood stage development. developed validated
strand-specific, non-polya-selected rna sequencing methods, pursued first
assembly p. falciparum strand-specific transcript structures rna
sequencing data. approach enabled annotation one thousand lncrna
transcript models comprehensive global analysis: coding prediction,
periodicity, stage-specificity, correlation, gc content, length, location
relative annotated transcripts, splicing. validated complete
splicing structure three lncrnas compelling properties.
non-polya-selected deep sequencing also enabled prediction hundreds of
intriguing p. falciparum circular rnas, six validated experimentally.
conclusions: found subset lncrnas, including subtelomeric
lncrnas, strongly peaked expression invasion. contrast, antisense
transcript levels significantly dropped invasion. compared to
neighboring mrnas, expression antisense-sense pairs significantly
anti-correlated blood stage development, indicating transcriptional
interference. also validated p. falciparum produces circrnas, is
notable given lack rna interference organism, discovered a
highly expressed, five-exon antisense rna poised regulate p. falciparum
gametocyte development 1 (pfgdv1), gene required early sexual commitment
events.
