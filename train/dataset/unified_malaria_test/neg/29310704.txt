background: insecticide resistance malaria vectors increasing threat 
vector control tools currently deployed endemic countries. resistance
management must integral part national malaria control programmes'
(nmcps) next strategic plans alleviate risk control failure. this
obviously require clear database insecticide resistance support 
development plan. present work gathers original data insecticide
resistance 2009 2015 across côte d'ivoire west africa.
methods: two approaches adopted build update resistance data in
the country. resistance monitoring conducted 2013 2015 35
sentinel sites across country using standard procedure of
susceptibility test adult mosquitoes. four insecticide families (pyrethroids, 
organochlorides, carbamates organophosphates) tested. addition to
this survey, also reviewed literature assemble existing data on
resistance 2009 2015.
results: high resistance levels pyrethroids, organochlorides carbamates
were widespread study sites whereas anopheles populations remained
susceptible organophosphates. three resistance mechanisms identified,
involving high allelic frequencies kdr l1014f mutation (range = 0.46-1),
relatively low frequencies ace-1 r (below 0.5) elevated activity of
insecticide detoxifying enzymes, mainly mixed function oxidases (mfo), esterase
and glutathione s-transferase (gst) almost study sites.
conclusion: detailed map resistance highlights urgent need develop
new vector control tools complement current long-lasting insecticidal nets
(llins) although yet unclear whether resistance mechanisms will
impact malaria transmission control. researchers, industry, stakeholders 
must urgently join forces develop alternative tools. then, nmcps must
strive develop effective tactics plans manage resistance keeping mind
country-specific context feasibility.
