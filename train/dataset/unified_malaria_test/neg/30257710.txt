erythrocyte-binding assays reveal higher binding plasmodium knowlesi duffy
binding protein human fya+/b+ erythrocytes fya+/b- erythrocytes.

fong my(1), cheong fw, lau yl(2).

author information: 
(1)department parasitology, faculty medicine, university malaya, kuala
lumpur, malaysia. fongmy@um.edu.my.
(2)department parasitology, faculty medicine, university malaya, kuala
lumpur, malaysia.

background: merozoite zoonotic plasmodium knowlesi invades human
erythrocytes via binding duffy binding protein (pkdbpαii) duffy
antigen eythrocytes. duffy antigen two immunologically distinct
forms, fya fyb. study, erythrocyte-binding assay used to
quantitatively determine compare binding level pkdbpαii fya+/b+ and
fya+/b- human erythrocytes.
results: erythrocyte-binding assay, binding level determined by
scoring number rosettes formed erythrocytes surrounding
transfected mammalian cos-7 cells expressed pkdbpαii. assay result
revealed significant difference binding level. number rosettes
scored fya+/b+ 1.64-fold higher fya+/b- (155.50 ± 34.32 
94.75 ± 23.16 rosettes, respectively; t(6) = -2.935, p = 0.026).
conclusions: erythrocyte-binding assay provided simple approach to
quantitatively determine binding level pkdbpαii erythrocyte duffy
antigen. using assay, pkdbpαii found display higher binding to
fya+/b+ erythrocytes fya+/b- erythrocytes.
