whole mitogenome sequences (mtdna) exploited insect ecology
studies, using molecular markers reconstruct phylogenies, infer 
phylogeographic relationships gene flow. recent anopheles phylogenomic
studies provided information regarding time deep lineage divergences 
within genus. report complete 15,393 bp mtdna sequences of
anopheles aquasalis, neotropical human malaria vector. comparing its
structure base composition relevant available anopheline
mitogenomes, high similarity conserved genomic features observed.
furthermore, 22 mtdna sequences comprising anopheline dipteran sibling
species analyzed reconstruct phylogenies estimate dates divergence
between taxa. phylogenetic analysis using complete mtdna sequences suggests 
a. aquasalis diverged anopheles albitarsis complex ~28 million years ago
(mya), ~38 mya anopheles darlingi. bayesian analysis suggests the
most recent ancestor nyssorhynchus anopheles + cellia extant ~83 mya, 
corroborating current estimates ~79-100 mya. additional sampling and
publication african, asian, north american anopheline mitogenomes would
improve resolution anopheles phylogeny clarify early continental
dispersal routes.
