background: quantitative real-time pcr (qpcr) commonly used method to
confirm diagnosis malaria differentiate recrudescence from
re-infection, especially clinical trials reference laboratories where
precise quantification critical. although anti-malarial drug discovery is
based vivo murine efficacy models, use molecular analysis been
limited. aim study develop qpcr valid methodology to
support pre-clinical anti-malarial models using filter papers maintain
material qpcr compare traditional methods.
methods: fta technology (whatman) rapid safe method extracting
nucleic acids blood. peripheral blood samples mice infected with
plasmodium berghei, p. yoelii, p. falciparum kept frozen samples as
spots fta cards. extracted genetic material types samples was
assessed quantification qpcr using sets specific primers specifically
designed plasmodium 18s rrna, ldh, cytb genes.
results: optimal conditions nucleic acid extraction fta cards and
qpcr amplification set up, confirmed suitable parasite
quantification using dna template storage room temperature as
long 26 months case p. berghei samples 52 months p.
falciparum p. yoelii. quality dna extracted fta cards for
gene sequencing microsatellite amplification also assessed.
conclusions: first study report suitability fta cards and
qpcr assay quantify parasite load samples vivo efficacy models to
support drug discovery process.
