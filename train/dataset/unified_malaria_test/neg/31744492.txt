background: malaria major public health problem china-myanmar border 
region. genetic structure malaria parasite may affect transmission
model control strategies. present study analyse genetic diversity 
of plasmodium falciparum merozoite surface proteins 1 2 (msp1 msp2)
and determine multiplicity infection clinical isolates the
china-myanmar border region.
methods: venous blood samples (172) filter paper blood spots (70) p.
falciparum isolates collected patients china-myanmar border 
region 2006 2011. genomic dna extracted, msp1 msp2
genes genotyped nested pcr using allele-specific primers p.
falciparum.
results: total 215 p. falciparum clinical isolates genotyped the
msp1 (201) msp2 (204), respectively. msp1 gene, mad20 family was
dominant (53.49%), followed k1 family (44.65%), ro33 family
(12.56%). msp2 gene, frequent allele fc27 family
(80.93%), followed 3d7 family (75.81%). total multiplicity of
infection (moi) msp1 msp2 1.76 2.21, prevalence 64.19%
and 72.09%, respectively. significant positive correlation moi 
parasite density found msp1 gene p. falciparum. sequence analysis
revealed 38 different alleles msp1 (14 k1, 23 mad20, 1 ro33) 52
different alleles msp2 (37 3d7 15 fc27).
conclusion: present study showed genetic polymorphisms diverse
allele types msp1 msp2 well high moi p. falciparum clinical
isolates china-myanmar border region.
