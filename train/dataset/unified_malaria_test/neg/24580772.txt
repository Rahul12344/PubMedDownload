background: apicomplexan parasites genus babesia, theileria plasmodium
are closely related organisms. interestingly, mitochondrial (mt)
genomes highly divergent. among babesia, babesia orientalis new species 
recently identified specifically epidemic southern part china,
causing severe disease water buffalo. however, information mt genome
of b. orientalis available.
methods: four pairs primers designed based full genome sequence of
b. orientalis (unpublished data) aligning reported mt genomes b. bovis,
b. bigemina, t. parva. entire mt genome amplified four sets of
pcr. obtained mt genome annotated aligning published apicomplexan
mt genomes artemis software v11. phylogenetic analysis performed using
cox1 cob amino acid sequences.
results: complete mt genome b. orientalis (wuhan strain) sequenced and
characterized. entire mt genome 5996 bp length linear form,
containing three protein-coding genes including cytochrome c oxidase (cox1),
cytochrome b (cob) cytochrome c oxidase iii (cox3) six rrna large subunit
gene fragments. gene arrangement b. orientalis mt genome similar to
those b. bovis, b. gibsoni theileria parva, different t.
orientalis, t. equi plasmodium falciparum. comparative analysis indicated
that cox1 cob genes conserved cox3. phylogenetic analysis
based amino acid sequences cox1, cob cox1 + cob, respectively, revealed
that b. orientalis fell babesia clade closest relationship b.
bovis.
conclusions: availability entire mt genome sequences b. orientalis 
provides valuable information future phylogenetic, population genetics and
molecular epidemiological studies apicomplexan parasites.
