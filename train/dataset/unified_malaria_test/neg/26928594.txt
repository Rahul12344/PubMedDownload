background: nested pcrs based plasmodium 18s-rrna gene been
extensively used human malaria diagnosis. however, practical
when large quantities samples need processed, been
challenges performance interpreting results, especially when
submicroscopic infections analysed. use "direct pcr" was
investigated aim improving diagnosis malaria elimination era.
methods: performance plasmodium cytochrome oxidase iii gene (cox-iii) 
based novel malaria detection strategies (direct nested pcr direct single
pcr) compared using 18s-rrna direct nested pcr reference tool.
evaluations based sensitivity, specificity ability detect
mixed infections using control blood spot samples field collected blood
samples final species diagnosis confirmation sequencing.
results: cox-iii direct pcr (limit detection: 0.6-2 parasites/μl) more
sensitive 18s-rrna direct nested pcr (limit detection: 2-10
parasites/μl). cox-iii direct pcr identified 21 positive controls (no
mixed infections detected) 18s-rrna direct nested pcr identified 18/21 
(including four mixed infections). different concentrations simulated mixed
infections (plasmodium vivax plasmodium falciparum) suggest cox-iii 
direct pcr detects predominant species. 18s-rrna direct nested 
pcr used detect plasmodium field collected bloods spots (n = 3833),
there discrepancy results genus pcr (16 % positive) the
species-specific pcr (5 % positive). further, large portion subset of
these positive samples (93 % genus 60 % p. vivax), align with
plasmodium sequences. contrast, cox-iii direct pcr clearly identified
(single bands confirmed sequencing) 2 % positive plasmodium samples
including p. vivax, p. falciparum, plasmodium malariae plasmodium ovale
wallikeri.
conclusions: cox-iii single direct pcr alternative method accurate 
detection plasmodium microscopic submicroscopic infections humans,
especially large number samples require screening. pcr not
require dna isolation, sensitive, quick, produces confident/clear results,
identifies plasmodium species infecting humans, cost-effective.
