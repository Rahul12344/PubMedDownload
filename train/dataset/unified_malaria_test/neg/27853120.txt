multiplex-touchdown pcr simultaneously detect cryptosporidium parvum, giardia

this study aimed develop multiplex-touchdown pcr method simultaneously
detect 3 species protozoan parasites, i.e., cryptosporidium parvum, giardia
lamblia, cyclospora cayetanensis, major causes traveler's diarrhea and
are resistant standard antimicrobial treatments. target genes included the
cryptosporidium oocyst wall protein c. parvum, glutamate dehydrogenase g.
lamblia, 18s ribosomal rna (18s rrna) c. cayetanensis. sizes the
amplified fragments 555, 188, 400 bps, respectively. the
multiplex-touchdown pcr protocol using primer mixture simultaneously detected
protozoa human stools, amplified gene detected >1×103 oocysts
for c. parvum, >1×104 cysts g. lamblia, >1 copy 18s rrna gene 
c. cayetanensis. taken together, protocol convincingly demonstrated the
ability simultaneously detect c. parvum, g. lamblia, c. cayetanenesis in
stool samples.
