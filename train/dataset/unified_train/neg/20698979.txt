background: injecting drug use increasingly important cause hiv
transmission countries worldwide, especially eastern europe, south
america, east southeast asia. among people actively injecting drugs,
provision clean needles opioid substitution reduce hiv-transmission.
however, former injecting drug users (fidus) often overlooked high risk 
group hiv transmission. compared hiv risk behavior among current and
former injecting drug users (idus) indonesia, rapidly growing
hiv-epidemic largely driven injecting drug use.
methods: current former idus recruited respondent driven sampling 
an urban setting java, interviewed regarding drug use hiv risk
behavior using european addiction severity index blood borne virus
transmission questionnaire. drug use hiv transmission risk behavior were
compared current idus former idus, using mann-whitney pearson
chi-square test.
results: ninety-two 210 participants (44%) self reported former idus.
risk behavior related sex, tattooing piercing common among current as
well former idus, 13% former idus still exposed contaminated
injecting equipment. hiv-infection high among former (66%) current (60%) 
idus.
conclusion: former idus may contribute significantly hiv-epidemic in
indonesia, hiv-prevention therefore also target group, addressing
sexual risk behavior.
