frequency viral, bacterial parasitic enteropathogens among young children 
with acute diarrhoea saudi arabia.

johargy a(1), ghazi h, mumenah a.

author information: 
(1)university manchester, manchester, united kingdom.

objective: frequency viral, bacterial parasitic enteropathogens among 
270 paediatric patients (< 5 years age) various hospitals makkah and
jeddah cities saudi arabia investigated.
methods: total 270 stool samples collected paediatric patients
with signs symptoms gastroenteritis different hospitals makkah and
jeddah cities saudi arabia. samples investigated bacterial, viral and
parasitic enteropathogens using microscopic examination, immunological tests 
bacterial culture techniques.
results: 270 stool samples tested study various causative
agents gastroenteritis, total number positive samples 106 (39%). of
these, 90 (33%) viral etiology, rotavirus type (serotype g) was
found majority cases 60 (22%), adenovirus 20 (7%) patients, and
astrovirus remaining 10 (4%) patients. thirteen (5%) bacterial
origins 9 (3%) salmonella species, 4 (2%) shigella species and
only 3 (1%) samples positive giardia lamblia.
conclusion: study indicated diarrhoeal diseases young
children saudi arabia due viral etiology, rotavirus was
predominant followed adenovirus astrovirus. salmonella shigella 
represent bacterial etiology paediatric acute diarrhoea giardia
lamblia found parasitic cause diarrhoea young children our
study.
