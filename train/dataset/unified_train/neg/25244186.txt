background: march 23, 2014, world health organization (who) notified
of outbreak ebola virus disease (evd) guinea. august 8, who
declared epidemic "public health emergency international concern."
methods: september 14, 2014, total 4507 probable confirmed cases,
including 2296 deaths evd (zaire species) reported five
countries west africa--guinea, liberia, nigeria, senegal, sierra leone. we
analyzed detailed subset data 3343 confirmed 667 probable ebola cases
collected guinea, liberia, nigeria, sierra leone september 14.
results: majority patients 15 44 years age (49.9% male), 
estimate case fatality rate 70.8% (95% confidence interval [ci], 69
to 73) among persons known clinical outcome infection. course of
infection, including signs symptoms, incubation period (11.4 days), and
serial interval (15.3 days), similar reported previous outbreaks of
evd. basis initial periods exponential growth, estimated
basic reproduction numbers (r0 ) 1.71 (95% ci, 1.44 2.01) guinea, 1.83
(95% ci, 1.72 1.94) liberia, 2.02 (95% ci, 1.79 2.26) sierra
leone. estimated current reproduction numbers (r) 1.81 (95% ci, 1.60 to
2.03) guinea, 1.51 (95% ci, 1.41 1.60) liberia, 1.38 (95% ci, 1.27
to 1.51) sierra leone; corresponding doubling times 15.7 days (95%
ci, 12.9 20.3) guinea, 23.6 days (95% ci, 20.2 28.2) liberia, and
30.2 days (95% ci, 23.6 42.3) sierra leone. assuming change the
control measures epidemic, november 2, 2014, cumulative reported 
numbers confirmed probable cases predicted 5740 guinea, 9890 
in liberia, 5000 sierra leone, exceeding 20,000 total.
conclusions: data indicate without drastic improvements control
measures, numbers cases deaths evd expected continue
increasing hundreds thousands per week coming months.
