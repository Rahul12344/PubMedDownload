background: injecting drug use primary mode hiv transmission and
acquisition afghanistan. people inject drugs (pwid) country have
been characterized high risk injecting behavior high burden hcv
infection. aimed estimate burden hiv, hcv, infectious
diseases identify correlates hiv hcv infection among pwid
living three major afghan cities 2009.
methods: epidemiologic data collected among pwid integrated
biological behavioral surveillance (ibbs) survey may august, 2009
in three afghan cities. data collected using structured questionnaire 
biologic specimens screen hiv, hbv, hcv, syphilis, hsv-2 using rapid
testing kits. multiple logistic regression models constructed identify
correlates infection.
results: among 548 participants, pooled hiv prevalence 7.1% (mazar-i-sharif: 
1.0%, kabul: 3.1%, herat: 18.4%) hcv prevalence 40.3%. almost all
participants hiv infection co-infected hcv (94.9%). pooled
prevalence estimates diseases included 7.1% hbv, 5.5% syphilis;
and 9.3% hsv-2. living herat, ever prison time injecting were
independently associated hiv infection. living kabul, herat time
injecting independently associated hcv infection.
conclusions: high heterogeneous burden hiv hcv among pwid
in afghan cities. provision comprehensive harm reduction services pwid in
afghanistan warranted reduce exposures associated hiv hcv
infection, especially city herat.
