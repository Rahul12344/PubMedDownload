background: china facing critical challenge rapid widespread human
immunodeficiency virus (hiv)/acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (aids) increase. 
rural-to-urban migration plays crucial role shifting hiv/sexual
transmitted infection (sti) epidemic. purpose study assess the
prevalence sexual behaviors correlates among early adolescents 
migrant workers china.
methods: cross-sectional study conducted 10 junior high schools from
april june 2008. total 2821 adolescents aged 14.06 +/- 0.93 years
(8.9% migrant workers vs. 91.1% general residents) participated the
survey. self-administrated questionnaire used collect information on
knowledge, attitude, behaviors associated increased risk hiv/sti.
results: percentage adolescents ever sexual intercourse had
sexual intercourse last three months 7.2% 4.3% adolescents of
migrant workers, respectively; contrast, 4.5% 1.8% peers of
general residents, respectively. 47.3% adolescents migrant workers 34.3%
of adolescents general residents reported condom use sexual
intercourse last three months. multivariate logistic regression analyses
found migration independent risk factor sexual intercourse last
three months sampled adolescents (odds ratio [or] = 1.23, 95% confidence
interval [ci]: 1.01-1.72). adolescents migrant workers, factors as
lower family income (or: 2.22, ci: 1.09-3.05 low level; or:1.25, ci:
1.04-1.59 medium level), younger age first sexual intercourse (or: 1.24,
ci: 1.09-1.57), lower knowledge hiv/aids (or: 0.93, ci: 0.90-0.97), fewer 
communication hiv/aids related issues (or: 0.79, ci: 0.90-0.97) related
to sexual intercourse last three months.
conclusion: based results, advocated heightened concerns
targeting adolescents migrant workers particularly necessary, given
their higher level sexual experience, lower socioeconomic status, restricted
reproductive health information, vulnerability hiv/sti.
