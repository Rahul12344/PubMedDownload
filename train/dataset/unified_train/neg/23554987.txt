prospective validation prognostic model respiratory syncytial virus
bronchiolitis late preterm infants: multicenter birth cohort study.

objectives: study aimed update validate prediction rule for
respiratory syncytial virus (rsv) hospitalization preterm infants 33-35 weeks 
gestational age (wga).
study design: risk study consisted 2 multicenter prospective birth cohorts
in 41 hospitals. risk factors assessed birth among healthy preterm
infants 33-35 wga. hospitalizations respiratory tract infection were
screened proven rsv infection immunofluorescence polymerase chain
reaction. multivariate logistic regression analysis used update an
existing prediction model derivation cohort (n = 1,227). validation
cohort (n = 1,194), predicted versus actual rsv hospitalization rates were
compared determine validity model.
results: rsv hospitalization risk cohorts comparable (5.7% versus
4.9%). derivation cohort, prediction rule determine probability of
rsv hospitalization developed using 4 predictors: family atopy (or 1.9;
95%ci, 1.1-3.2), birth period (or 2.6; 1.6-4.2), breastfeeding (or 1.7; 1.0-2.7) 
and siblings daycare attendance (or 4.7; 1.7-13.1). model showed good
discrimination (c-statistic 0.703; 0.64-0.76, 0.702 bootstrapping).
external validation showed good discrimination calibration (c-statistic
0.678; 0.61-0.74).
conclusions: prospectively validated prediction rule identifies infants at
increased rsv hospitalization risk, may benefit targeted preventive
interventions. prediction rule facilitate country-specific,
cost-effective use rsv prophylaxis late preterm infants.
