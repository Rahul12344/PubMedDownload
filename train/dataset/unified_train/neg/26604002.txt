and nonhuman primates. study, identified novel protoparvovirus,
designated ratbuv, intestinal contents wild rats using viral
metagenomics. near complete genome 4643 nt encoding ns1, vp1, vp2
proteins. phylogenetic analysis complete genome showed ratbuv
clustered mpulungu buv shrews. sequence analysis indicated the
putative protein sequences ns1, vp1, vp2 ratbuv shared identities of
50.6-77.2, 48.3-77.3, 47.1-78.3 %, respectively, human buvs,
mpbuv, wuharv parvovirus, suggesting ratbuv belongs new species of
protoparvovirus. epidemiologic study indicated prevalence rate of
ratbuv cohort 40 wild rats 12.5 % (5/40), higher 
of buv humans previous study.
