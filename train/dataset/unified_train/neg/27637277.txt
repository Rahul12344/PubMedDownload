large retropharyngeal abscess: case mistaken identity.

makaram n(1), gohil r(1), gardiner q(1), manickavasagam j(1).

author information: 
(1)department otorhinolaryngology, ninewells hospital, dundee, uk.

we report unusual case nasopharyngeal carcinoma (npc), presenting initially
as retropharyngeal collection. discuss investigation, diagnosis and
management npc furthermore review literature npc, emphasising the
varied presentation. 44-year-old caucasian male ex-smoker presented a
10-day history painless left-sided neck lump; progressive dysphagia and
unintentional weight loss. examination demonstrated large left posterior
pharyngeal swelling soft palatal effacement, confirmed nasoendoscopy. a
ct scan revealed fluid collection retropharyngeal space, partially
occluded nasopharynx, numerous satellite lesions identified along
with cervical lymphadenopathy. suspected abscess drained theatre and
nasopharyngeal biopsies taken. revealed epstein-barr virus-positive
npc. staging investigations revealed t4n2m0l carcinoma. treatment consisted 
radical chemoradiation therapy curative intent. npc known varied 
and late presentation, important condition aware when
considering differential diagnosis pharyngeal masses.
