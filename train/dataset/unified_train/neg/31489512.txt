purpose: describe knowledge human papillomavirus (hpv), hpv-related care
behavior, advocacy intent (e.g., vaccine recommendation willingness to
become advocate vaccination) investigate associations between
knowledge, hpv-related care behavior, advocacy intent among hpv-related
cancer survivors.
methods: cross-sectional online survey offered qualtrics to
hpv-related cancer survivors either volunteers cancer center or
patients survivorship clinics.
results: total 200 survivors responded. 33.2% respondents reported
knowing cancer hpv-related 56.8% reported hpv vaccine safe.
participants knew cancer caused hpv likely to
have vaccinated children (p < .001). also, participants knew the
vaccine safe willing recommend vaccine (p < .001), a
peer mentor others hpv-related cancers (43.2% vs. 14.0%, p < .001), 
to act advocate increasing vaccination rates (44.1% vs. 24.4%,
p = 0.01). finally, survivors aware vaccine's effectiveness in
decreasing precancerous lesions likely recommend vaccine (45.7% 
vs. 12.0%, p = .002).
conclusions: raising survivor awareness link hpv cancer and
hpv vaccine safety may increase willingness serve powerful opinion
leaders peer mentors promote hpv vaccination. providers may take the
simple step informing patients cancer hpv-related hpv
vaccine safe increase number informed empowered survivors.
