[occupational exposure blood among medical students casablanca (morocco):
analysis knowledge practices].

[article french]

berahou h, serhier z, housbane s, ajbal k, bennani othmani m.

occupational exposure blood (oeb) associated significant risk of
transmission bloodborne viruses medical students. objective this
study evaluate knowledge practices casablanca medical students 
concerning oeb preventive measures behaviour accidental exposure.
a cross-sectional study carried ibn rochd university hospital in
casablanca using stratified cluster sampling ; data study were
collected means anonymous self-administered questionnaire. mean age 
was 22.38 years (sd = 1.69) female predominance (56.0%). 17.5% of
students experienced least one oeb, 38.3% familiar 
standard precautions prevent accidents. accidental exposure was
significantly associated needle recapping (p = 0.002) ignorance of
standard precautions (p = 0.028).these results illustrate insufficient
knowledge poor compliance preventive measures occupational exposure
to blood, indicating need awareness-raising training activities for
medical students.
