
1. ophthalmology. 2002 jun;109(6):1142-7.

bilateral ophthalmic artery occlusion patient acquired immunodeficiency
syndrome central nervous system lymphoma.

bachman dm(1), green wr, holman r.

author information: 
(1)department ophthalmology, washington hospital center, washington, dc, usa.

purpose: clinical course autopsy findings patient human
immunodeficiency virus-1 immunodeficiency, central nervous system lymphoma, and
bilateral, simultaneous ophthalmic artery occlusions.
design: observational case report.
methods: clinical examination, fundus photography, gross microscopic
pathologic study.
results: fundus photographs disclosed stasis retinal arterioles, absence
of cherry-red spot; internal carotid arteriography disclosed bilateral
ophthalmic artery occlusions; postmortem histopathologic examination disclosed
bilateral ophthalmic artery atherosclerosis, retinal ischemic necrosis, ischemic 
optic neuropathy, diffuse large-cell lymphoma multiple areas central
nervous system, cytomegalovirus encephalitis, atherosclerosis, and
bronchopneumonia.
conclusions: 47-year-old male acquired immunodeficiency syndrome, profound
immunodeficiency, systemic hypertension, central nervous system lymphoma,
developed deep vein thrombosis, bilateral ophthalmic artery occlusions, died 
of pneumonia 7 weeks onset blindness. postmortem study revealed
bilateral ophthalmic artery hemorrhagic atherosclerosis, ischemic optic
neuropathy, ischemic retinal necrosis, diffuse large-cell central nervous system 
lymphoma, cytomegalovirus encephalitis, pneumonitis, systemic
atherosclerosis.
