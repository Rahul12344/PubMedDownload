an unusual presentation cardiac tamponade associated epstein-barr virus
infection.

ho km(1), mitchell sc(1).

author information: 
(1)department care older people, whittington hospital, london, uk.

we present case 80-year-old woman presented dry cough,
shortness breath general malaise. presented 5 days previously with
a sore throat, feeling feverish non-specific symptoms. background
included paroxysmal atrial fibrillation hypertension. initially
thought decompensated heart failure secondary atrial fibrillation 
was initiated diuretic therapy. however, transthoracic echocardiogram
performed revealed collapse right ventricle swinging heart,
suggestive cardiac tamponade. patient underwent therapeutic
pericardiocentesis 700 ml exudative, blood-stained fluid drained.
subsequent testing pericardial fluid revealed presence epstein-barr
virus dna. serial follow-up transthoracic echocardiograms revealed resolution 
the pericardial effusion patient remained asymptomatic.
