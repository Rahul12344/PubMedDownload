the authors report fatal outcome 4-year-old boy herpes simplex virus 
(hsv) pneumonia ependymoma. patient respiratory distress that
worsened despite antibiotic treatment. bronchoalveolar lavage showed intranuclear
viral inclusions, culture positive hsv type 1. t-cell count was
significantly decreased. although acyclovir foscarnet given, patient
died. postmortem examination showed hsv pneumonitis severe alveolar damage
and severe involutional changes thymus absence hassall's
corpuscles. hsv must considered differential diagnosis patients with
interstitial pneumonia t-cell deficiency, especially craniospinal
irradiation.
