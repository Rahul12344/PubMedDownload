background: switzerland, incidence hepatitis c virus (hcv) infection in
hiv-positive men sex men (msm) rose 18-fold 1998 2011. 
we aimed evaluate transmission risk factors, awareness, seroprevalence 
hcv among msm southwest switzerland.
methods: 1st june 2011 31st august 2012, trained health care
professionals invited individuals attending (1) msm screening clinics (2)
indoor outdoor meeting areas complete anonymous questionnaire.
consenting participants rapid tested hcv (oraquick hcv rapid antibody
test).
results: 918 msm approached, 654 agreed participate, (536,
82%) enrolled via msm screening clinics. 654 participants, 21 (3.2%)
disclosed hiv positive; 140 (21%) unknown hiv status. preceding 
12 months, 357 (55%) 654 participants reported unprotected anal intercourse
(uai) 321 (49%) 654 participants reported uai partners of
different/unknown hiv status. hiv serosorting reported frequently
among hiv-positive individuals (76%, p < 0.001). three hundred two participants
(46%) aware hcv, awareness higher among clinic meeting area
participants (49% vs. 33%, p = 0.04). one individual (of 654; 0.2%), a
negative hiv test result 18 months previously newly diagnosed hcv
positive rapid testing.
conclusions: sample predominantly hiv-negative msm, half the
participants aware hcv hcv seroprevalence low. however, high
rates uai uai without hiv serosorting reported. given the
increasing incidence hcv among hiv-positive men, propose hcv
counseling offered msm regardless hiv status, testing
offered high risk.
