this study reports seroprevalence bovine viral infections llamas (lama 
glama) argentina. first study made country including 390
llamas testing antibodies eight viruses. samples collected 
nine farms distributed three different provinces: buenos aires, córdoba and
jujuy. samples tested antibodies eight viruses known to
infect cattle: bovine herpesvirus type 1 (bhv-1), bovine viral diarrhea virus
(bvdv), bovine adenovirus type 3 (badv iii), bovine enterovirus (bev), bovine
rotavirus (brv), bluetongue virus (btv), bovine leukemia virus (blv), and
foot-and-mouth disease virus (fmdv). antibody prevalences detected llamas 
were: bhv-1 0.7% samples (3/390), bvdv 2.0% (8/390), badv iii in
5.1% (20/390), bev 4.6% (18/390), brv 87.6% (342/390). antibodies were
detected btv, blv viaa (viral infection associated antigen) of
the analyzed camelids.
