methods: male subjects (n=375) aged 18-70 years, attending std clinic were
recruited. urethral swabs assessed hpv dna using polymerase chain
reaction (pcr) consensus primers my09/11. hpv genotypes positive pcr 
products determined restriction fragment length polymorphisms direct 
sequence analysis.
results: 375 swabs collected, 305 (81.3%) yielded sufficient dna the
subsequent hpv analysis. among 305 subjects, prevalence hpv 13.8%.
nononcogenic hpv types found 8.5% (26/305) subjects, oncogenic types
in 4.3% (13/305), multiple types 1.0% (3/305). prevalence hpv
infection higher subjects urban area rural area (p
< 0.05). prevalence also higher received fewer years of
education (p < 0.05) sex partners (p < 0.05).
conclusions: hpv infection among men high risk uncommon. detection 
rate hpv dna significantly related sociodemographic factors, such
