background: comorbid depression hepatitis b virus (hbv) common. developing 
accurate time- efficient tools measure depressive symptoms hbv is
important research clinical practice china.
aims: study tested psychometric properties chinese version the
16-item quick inventory depressive symptomatology (qids-sr) hbv patients.
methods: study recruited 245 depressed patients hbv related liver
disease. severity depressive symptoms assessed the
montgomery-asberg depression rating scale (madrs) qids-sr.
results: internal consistency (cronbach's alpha) 0.796 qids-sr. the
qids-sr total score significantly correlated madrs total score
(r=0.698, p<0.001). qids-sr showed unidimensional measurement properties in
exploratory factor analysis.
conclusions: qids-sr (chinese version) good psychometric properties in
hbv patients appears useful assessing depression clinical
settings.
