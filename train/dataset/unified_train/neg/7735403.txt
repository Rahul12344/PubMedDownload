objective: characterize urban street youth population, self-reported 
rates drug use, involvement behaviors put risk for
infection human immunodeficiency virus.
design: brief structured interview administered 409 youths 
living streets 2 consecutive months, fully
integrated "street economy."
setting: thirty percent sample recruited community-based service
sites 70% recruited street locations natural hangouts.
participants: youths aged 12 23 years; 74% male, 48% ethnic
minorities, 72% homeless, 14% gang affiliated, 20% involved in
drug dealing, 43% engaged survival sex (ie, exchange sexual
favor money, food, place stay, clothes, and/or drugs), 40% were
homosexual bisexual.
results: seventy percent youths sexually active, average of
11.7 sexual partners (past 30 days). youths multiple sexual partners were
more likely previous sexually transmitted disease (p < .01), use
drugs sex (p < .001), involved survival sex (p < .001).
marijuana (55%), methamphetamine (62%), crack (38%) drugs choice,
with 30% sample reporting injecting drug use (58% subset reported 
injecting drug use within past 30 days). substance-abusing youth 3.6
times likely use drugs sex, 2.2 times likely engage in
survival sex, 2.5 times likely diagnosed a
sexually transmitted disease.
conclusions: high-risk sexual drug use behaviors prevalent and
interrelated urban street youth sample. suggests need new
and innovative educational promotions prevention interventions targeted to
this population.
