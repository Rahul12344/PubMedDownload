a seven-year-old horse euthanised exhibiting severe rapidly
progressive neurological disorder. tissue samples despatched italian 
reference centre animal foreign diseases (istituto 'g. caporale' teramo)
for diagnosis. laboratory tests equine neurotropic viruses gave negative 
results. scattered perivascular inflammatory infiltrates several parasites
that morphologically classified halicephalobus gingivalis, seen
within brain upon microscopic examination. pathological findings led the
diagnosis parasitic meningo-encephalitis caused h. gingivalis. case
report confirms halicephalobosis taken account the
differential diagnosis equine encephalopathy also highlights value 
of multidisciplinary approach problem solving veterinary medicine.
