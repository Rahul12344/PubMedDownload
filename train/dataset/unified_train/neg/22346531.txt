disseminated nocardia otitidiscaviarum patient aids.

sandre rm(1), summerbell rc.

author information: 
(1)haven (hiv) clinic, laurentian hospital, sudbury, ontario; ontario ministry of
health, laboratory services branch, etobicoke, ontario; university toronto,
department clinical biochemistry, toronto, ontario.

a case disseminated infection due nocardia otitidiscaviarum described in
a caucasian man infected human immunodeficiency virus. patient
presented previous aids-defining conditions, cd4 lymphocyte count of
206 cells/mm(3) enlarging intra-abdominal chest wall abscesses with
bilateral pulmonary infiltrates. aggressive surgical debridement and
antimicrobial therapy trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole amikacin resulted in
clinical cure. long term suppressive therapy needed prevent relapse.
