following large west nile virus (wnv) epidemic northeastern italy 2008,
human animal surveillance activities implemented emilia romagna.
human surveillance performed serology genome detection blood and
cerebrospinal fluid suspected cases suffering acute
meningoencephalitis regional territory. animal surveillance consisted of
passive active surveillance horses active surveillance wild birds
and mosquitoes. 15 june 31 october 2009, nine 78 possible cases of
west nile neuroinvasive disease confirmed (three fatal). may to
october, 26 cases neurological west nile disease confirmed among 46
horses. overall incidence seroconversion among horses 2009 13%. 
2009, 44 1,218 wild birds yielded positive pcr results wnv infection. 
planned veterinary entomological surveillance actions detected wnv activity
from end july 2009, 2-3 weeks onset first human
neurological case. passive surveillance horses seems early and
suitable tool detection wnv activity, less sensitive in
the future, intensive programme horse vaccination started june
2009.
