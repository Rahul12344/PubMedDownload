author information: 
(1)india hiv/aids alliance, sarovar center, secretariat road, hyderabad, andhra

objective: anal sex among heterosexual couples rise reported 
many scientific studies. considering unprotected anal sex higher risk of
human immunodeficiency virus (hiv) transmission vaginal sex, we
undertook study understand anal sex practices among female sex workers
(fsw).
materials methods: study conducted among fsw attending 11 randomly
selected sexually transmitted infection (sti) clinics bill melinda gates
supported targeted interventions andhra pradesh. structured questionnaire
was administered 555 fsw attending clinics project clinic
counselors. informed consent obtained study participants.
results: engaging anal sex self reported 22% sex workers, though
demand clients reported much higher (40%). reasons anal
sex practices included money (61%), clout/influence client (45%),
risk losing client (27%), forced sex (1.2%). factors associated anal 
sex higher number clients, higher duration sex work, higher income,
and older age group. associated risks perceived fsw bleeding injury
to anal canal (98%) 28% associated higher hiv transmission
risk. reported condom lubricant use 88% 39% respectively.
conclusion: study shows frequent anal sex, inconsistent condom 
and infrequent lubricant usage, economic physical coercion, low awareness
of sti/hiv transmission risk among fsw, serious implications hiv
prevention programmes. need focus anal sex education use 
lubricants along condoms anal sex fsw-targeted interventions in
ap.
