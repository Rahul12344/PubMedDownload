rift valley fever (rvf) major zoonotic arboviral hemorrhagic fever. 
conditions leading rvf epidemics still unclear, relative role of
climatic anthropogenic factors may vary ecosystems. here, estimate
the likely scenario led rvf emergence island mayotte,
following 2006-2007 african epidemic. developed first mathematical
model rvf accounts climate, animal imports livestock
susceptibility, fitted 12-years dataset. rvf emergence found to
be triggered import infectious animals, whilst transmissibility was
approximated linear exponential function vegetation density. model
forecasts indicated low probability virus endemicity 2017, and
therefore re-emergence closed system (i.e. without import infected
animals). however, high proportion naive animals reached 2016
implies island remains vulnerable import infectious animals.
we recommend reinforcing surveillance livestock, rvf reported is
neighbouring territories. model tested elsewhere, with
ecosystem-specific data.
