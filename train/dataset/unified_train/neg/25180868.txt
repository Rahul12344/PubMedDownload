[influence liver function pregnancy outcome women hepatitis b virus
who receive individualized nutritional management].

[article chinese]

luo x(1), dong x, xu m.

author information: 
(1)department obstetrics gynecology, second affiliate hospital,
chongqing medical university, chongqing 400010, china.

objective: evaluate influence liver function pregnancy outcome of
women infected hepatitis b virus (hbv) receive
individualized nutrition management perinatal period.
methods: total 210 pregnant women enrolled study january
2011 july 2013. women divided following groups according to
self-selected participation individualized nutritional treatment and
management plans (n =87, observation group) routine prenatal care without
any nutrition management plan (n =123, control group). inter-group differences in
pregnancy complications, liver function perinatal outcome assessed
statistically.
results: following pregnancy complications recorded the
study:hypertension disorder, gestational diabetes, anemia fetal growth
restriction; however, rates complications significantly lower
in observation group control group (p less 0.05). the
observation group also significantly lower incidence rates preterm labor, 
fetal distress neonatal asphyxia (p less 0.05). observation group
also significantly lower proportions hypoalbuminemia abnormal liver
function, correlation analysis indicated birth weight correlated with
the pregnant woman's energy protein intake.
conclusion: women hbv benefit individualized nutrition management
during prenatal period, experiencing improved pregnancy outcome reduced
occurrence pregnancy complications. application individualized and
balanced nutrition management effect fetal growth development.
