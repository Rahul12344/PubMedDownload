we experienced case chronic active epstein-barr virus infection (caebv)
complicated systemic lupus erythematosus (sle) antiphospholipid antibody
syndrome (aps). 35-year-old woman admitted hospital complaints 
of fever dyspnea exertion. diagnosed sle basis of
arthritis, oropharyngeal ulcer, lymphopenia, positive autoantibodies 
dna, rnp ssa. diagnosis aps also made positive
anti-cardiolipin igg antibodies existence multiple pulmonary
infarction pulmonary hypertension. administration 30 mg/day of
prednisolone anti-coagulation significantly improved clinical symptoms.
however, admitted hospital four months later of
progressive liver damage pancytopenia. increment prednisolone not
improve clinical situation expired pulmonary hemorrhage.
at autopsy, significant increase histiocytes with
hemophagocytosis dense infiltration atypical lymphocytes liver,
spleen, lymph nodes bone marrow. infiltrated lymphocytes positive cd
3 eber 1 immunohistochemical staining ebvmrna detected situ 
hybridization. final pathological diagnosis caebv hemophagocytic
syndrome association lupus nephritis, pulmonary hemorrhage pulmonary 
infarction.
