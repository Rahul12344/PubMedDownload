background: couples man infected human immunodeficiency virus
(hiv) increasingly request assisted reproductive technology (art) allow safe
procreation. semen quality critical situations.
methods: semen characteristics evaluated 189 hiv-infected men requesting 
art. time semen analysis men healthy 177 receiving
anti-retroviral therapy. comparisons made hiv-seronegative men,
partners women requiring ivf tubal infertility, matching 
age sexual abstinence delay.
results: significant semen alterations found hiv-infected men
were reduced percentages rapidly progressive sperm [median (range), 10%
(0-30%) compared 15% (5-30%) controls, p < 0.001], increased
concentrations non-spermatic cells [3 x 10(6)/ml (0.2-16 x 10(6)/ml) compared 
with 1.1 x 10(6)/ml (0.1-14 x 10(6)/ml) controls, p < 0.001]. hiv-infected
men also showed lower ejaculate volumes [2.8 ml (0.6-9.3 ml) compared 3.6 ml
(1.1-11 ml), p < 0.05] total sperm counts [262.5 x 10(6) (0-1003 x 10(6))
compared 310.5 x 10(6) (48.3-1679 x 10(6)), p < 0.05].
conclusions: semen evaluation large population hiv-infected men
requesting art evidenced several alterations. anomalies might be
related anti-retroviral treatments.
