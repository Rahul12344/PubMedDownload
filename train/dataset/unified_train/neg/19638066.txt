anaplastic kaposi sarcoma (ks) uncommon histologic phenotype kaposi's
and one typically associated locally aggressive clinical course.
we report case 53-year-old human immunodeficiency virus-positive male, 
highly active antiretroviral therapy 1 month prior admission, presented
with fever, cough, respiratory distress, multiple skin lesions cervical and
inguinal lymphadenopathy responding multiple antibiotics. microscopic
examination punch biopsies forehead chest revealed spindled
cell neoplasm marked cytologic atypia scattered mitoses, features
consistent diagnosis anaplastic ks confirmed by
immunohistochemistry hhv-8. biopsy involved lymph node also revealed
involvement ks. despite aggressive clinical treatment, patient rapidly
deteriorated expired 1 week diagnosis anaplastic ks was
rendered. case underscores aggressive clinical course uncommon
histologic variant ks recalcitrant clinical behavior.
