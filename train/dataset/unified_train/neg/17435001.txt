uv light inactivation mycobacterium avium subsp. paratuberculosis in
middlebrook 7h9 broth whole semiskim milk investigated using a
laboratory-scale uv machine incorporated static mixers within uv-penetrable 
pipes. uv treatment proved less effective killing m. avium subsp.
paratuberculosis suspended milk (0.5- 1.0-log(10) reduction per 1,000
mj/ml) suspended middlebrook 7h9 broth (2.5- 3.3-log(10)
reduction per 1,000 mj/ml). fastplaquetb phage assay provided rapid
enumeration surviving m. avium subsp. paratuberculosis (within 24 h) than
culture herrold's egg yolk medium (6 8 weeks). despite fact plaque
counts consistently 1 2 log(10) lower colony counts throughout the
study, uv inactivation rates m. avium subsp. paratuberculosis derived using
the phage assay culture results significantly different (p = 0.077).
