persistent flank pain, low-grade fever, malaise woman treated with
indinavir.

sarcletti m(1), zangerle r.

author information: 
(1)department dermatology venereology, university innsbruck, austria.

this case report describes 32-year-old woman treated indinavir who
developed mild moderate flank pain, malaise, low-grade fever. sterile
pyuria preceded increased serum creatinine levels. workup revealed persistent
pyuria, normal-sized kidneys, normal intravenous pyelography, negative
urinary cultures. renal biopsy showed interstitial nephritis chronic
inflammation. collecting ducts contained crystals. two months treatment
with indinavir discontinued, serum creatinine levels returned normal and
pyuria disappeared. sterile pyuria patients taking indinavir may help to
identify patients risk renal dysfunction interstitial nephritis.
markedly increasing fluid intake recommended dosage may ameliorate 
or even reverse process tubulointerstitial disease.
