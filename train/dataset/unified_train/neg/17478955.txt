context: violence women global phenomenon cuts across all
social economic classes.
aims: study designed measure prevalence correlates of
domestic violence (dv) among women seeking services voluntary counseling and
testing (vct) center bangalore, india.
settings designs: cross-sectional survey conducted among women visiting
an human immunodeficiency virus (hiv) vct center bangalore, september 
and november 2005.
materials methods: interviewer-administered questionnaire used to
collect information violence variables.
statistical analysis used: univariable associations dv made using
pearson chi-squared test categorical variables student t-test the
mann-whitney test continuous variables.
results: forty-two percent respondents reported dv, including physical abuse
(29%), psychological abuse (69%) sexual abuse (1%). among women who
reported violence kind, 67% also reported hiv seropositive.
the common reasons reported dv included financial problems (38%),
husband's alcohol use (29%) woman's hiv status (18%). older women (p < 0.001)
and low income levels likely experienced dv (p =
0.02). factors included husband's education, hiv seropositivity alcohol
or tobacco use (p < 0.001).
conclusion: study found dv levels comparable studies around
the world. findings highlight need additional training among health
care providers vct centers screening dv, detection signs physical
abuse provisions referrals women suffering domestic partner
violence.
