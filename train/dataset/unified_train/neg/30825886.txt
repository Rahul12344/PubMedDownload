papillomaviruses among widespread animal viruses, many hosts
harbouring multiple virus types. present study aimed evaluate the
haematological immunophenotypic profile cattle infected bovine
papillomavirus (bpv). blood samples collected 10 animals clinical 
cutaneous bpv without clinical papillomatosis (control). haematological
analysis demonstrated significant reduction haemoglobin haematocrit 
bpv-infected animals. results also showed increase natural killer cells
and decrease γδ+ t-cells cd4+/cd8+ ratio bpv group when
compared control group. infection also found stimulate a
pro-inflammatory profile participation cd8+t cells producing elevated
ifn-γ il-17. findings, although preliminary, provide better
understanding immune response cattle infected bpv.
