background: although chikungunya virus rapidly expanded several countries 
in sub-saharan africa, little attention paid control and
management. recently, chikungunya regarded benign and
self-limiting disease. report describe first case severe
chikungunya disease adult patient pemba, mozambique.
case presentation: previously healthy 40 year old male makonde ethnicity
with known past medical history resident pemba past 11 years
presented severe febrile illness. despite administration broad spectrum
intravenous antibiotics patient rapidly deteriorated became comatose
while developing anaemia, thrombocytopenia later, melaena. laboratory testing
revealed igm antibodies chikungunya virus. malaria tests were
consistently negative.
conclusions: report suggests chikungunya might cause unsuspected severe
disease febrile patients mozambique provides insights the
improvement national protocols management febrile patients in
mozambique. recommend clinicians consider chikungunya the
differential diagnosis febrile illness locations aedes aegypti
mosquitos abundant.
