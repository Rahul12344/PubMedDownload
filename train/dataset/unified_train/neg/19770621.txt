objective: estimate effect combined antiretroviral therapy (cart) on
mortality among hiv-infected individuals appropriate adjustment for
time-varying confounding indication.
design: collaboration 12 prospective cohort studies europe the
united states (the hiv-causal collaboration) includes 62 760 hiv-infected,
therapy-naive individuals followed average 3.3 years. inverse
probability weighting marginal structural models used adjust for
measured confounding indication.
results: two thousand thirty-nine individuals died follow-up. 
mortality hazard ratio 0.48 (95% confidence interval 0.41-0.57) cart
initiation versus initiation. analyses stratified cd4 cell count at
baseline, corresponding hazard ratios 0.29 (0.22-0.37) less 100
cells/microl, 0.33 (0.25-0.44) 100 less 200 cells/microl, 0.38
(0.28-0.52) 200 less 350 cells/microl, 0.55 (0.41-0.74) 350 to
less 500 cells/microl, 0.77 (0.58-1.01) 500 cells/microl more.
the estimated hazard ratio varied years since initiation cart 0.57
(0.49-0.67) less 1 year since initiation 0.21 (0.14-0.31) 5 years
or (p value trend <0.001).
conclusion: estimated cart halved average mortality rate in
hiv-infected individuals. mortality reduction greater worse
prognosis start follow-up.
