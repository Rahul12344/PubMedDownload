[noninvasive positive-pressure ventilation elderly woman acute
respiratory failure caused influenza virus pneumonia].

a 79-year-old woman admitted january 18, 2000, lumbar compression
fracture. two days admission, chest x-ray film showed reticular
infiltrates right lung field. diagnosed pneumonia and
was treated antibiotics. despite therapy, symptoms improve 
and infiltrates diffusely extended involve lungs chest x-ray
films. placed noninvasive positive pressure ventilation (nippv) for
progressive respiratory failure 5 days admission order avoid
endotracheal intubation. hypoxemia immediately improved oronasal
bilevel positive airway pressure ventilation (bipap) chest x-ray films
revealed improvement reticular infiltrates. measurement viral antibody 
titers showed cause acute respiratory failure influenza virus
pneumonia. conclusion, nippv may reduce need intubation elderly
patients acute respiratory failure, well chronic obstructive pulmonary
disease (copd) patients.
