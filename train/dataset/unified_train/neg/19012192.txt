objectives: ascertain rate influenza immunization pregnant couples
whose infants required neonatal intensive care unit (nicu) admission, to

study design: 2005-07 influenza seasons, parents nicu patients at
two level iii centers surveyed tiv infant's nicu

gestational age, multiple births also obtained.
results: seven hundred eighty-six parents infants nicu. five
hundred forty-seven (69.5%) surveyed. overall parental rate of
vaccination 23.2% (127/547). parental vaccination rate differ
between years (23.2% vs. 23.2%). mothers less likely received tiv
prior delivery fathers (17.0% vs. 28.4%; p < 0.02). population,
19.3% received immunization ob/gyn. concordance rate 8.9% was
noted married couples receiving tiv. parents received tiv january
and february influenza seasons november december (p < 0.05,
student's t-test). influenza immunization rate nicu parents unrelated to
infant's gestational age, parental age, total number risk factors tiv,
hospital length stay, multiple gestation, need high risk obstetrical
care.
conclusions: tiv rates among high-risk pregnant parents whose infants are
admitted nicu lower expected compared general population.
