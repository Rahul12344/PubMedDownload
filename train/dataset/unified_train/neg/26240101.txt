addison's disease patient hypothyroidism: autoimmune polyglandular
syndrome type 2.

bain a(1), stewart m(2), mwamure p(2), nirmalaraj k(2).

author information: 
(1)university auckland, auckland, new zealand.
(2)tauranga hospital, tauranga, bay plenty, new zealand.

a 57-year-old caucasian woman known autoimmune hypothyroidism diagnosed in
2006 presented hospital flu-like symptoms circulatory collapse. she
reported weight loss gradual increase skin pigmentation 1-year 
period. aggressive fluid resuscitation instituted. hormonal tests showed
primary adrenal insufficiency. appropriate steroid replacement started with
rapid clinical response. subsequent antibody tests confirmed diagnosis of
autoimmune polyglandular type 2 (schmidt's) syndrome. adrenal crisis been
precipitated influenza virus type b infection.
