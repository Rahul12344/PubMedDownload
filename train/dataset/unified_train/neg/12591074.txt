this first case report gastric mucosal-associated lymphoid tissue
lymphoma adult cell leukemia/lymphoma (atll) cell infiltration. a
43-yr-old japanese woman seropositive antihuman cell leukemia
virus-i antibody complained epigastric pain april, 1996. endoscopy showed
gastric ulcers antrum. biopsy specimens showed helicobacter pylori
infection. symptoms relieved treatment ranitidine. march,
1998, complained epigastric pain abdominal fullness. smears of
peripheral blood revealed atypical lymphocytes nuclear irregularity,
consistent atll cells. diagnosed atll. endoscopy revealed
multiple gastric ulcers antrum angle. biopsy specimens
demonstrated small centrocyte-like cells forming lymphoepithelial lesions, with
infiltrations large atypical lymphoid cells atll. immunohistochemical
staining, small centrocyte-like cells positive b cell markers (l26, 
cd20), whereas large atypical lymphoid cells positive cell marker 
(uchl-1, cd45ro). findings attributed gastric mucosal-associated
lymphoid tissue lymphoma gastric involvement atll.
