dendritic cells (dcs) targets measles virus (mv) play central roles in
viral dissemination. however, dcs express rig-i-like receptors (rlrs) rig-i
and mda5 sense mv induce type interferon (ifn) production. given the
potency antiviral response, rlrs tightly regulated various steps, 
including dephosphorylation pp1 phosphatases, induces activation. 
we demonstrate mv suppresses rig-i mda5 activating c-type lectin 
dc-sign inducing signaling prevents rlr dephosphorylation. mv binding to
dc-sign leads activation kinase raf-1, induces association of
pp1 inhibitor i-1 gadd34-pp1 holoenzymes, thereby inhibiting phosphatase
activity. consequently, gadd34-pp1 holoenzymes unable dephosphorylate
rig-i mda5, hence suppressing type ifn responses enhancing mv
replication. blocking dc-sign signaling allows rlr activation suppresses mv
infection dcs. thus, mv subverts dc-sign control rlr activation escape 
antiviral responses.
