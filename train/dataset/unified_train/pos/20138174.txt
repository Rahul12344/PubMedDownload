bs69 cooperates traf3 regulation epstein-barr virus-derived
lmp1/ctar1-induced nf-kappab activation.

ikeda o(1), miyasaka y, yoshida r, mizushima a, oritani k, sekine y, kuroda m,
yasui t, fujimuro m, muromoto r, nanbo a, matsuda t.

author information: 
(1)department immunology, graduate school pharmaceutical sciences, hokkaido
university, sapporo 060-0812, japan.

epstein-barr virus latent membrane protein 1 (lmp1) activates nf-kappab signaling
pathways two c-terminal regions, ctar1 ctar2. previous studies have
demonstrated bs69, multidomain cellular protein, regulates
lmp1/ctar2-mediated nf-kappab activation interfering complex
formation tradd lmp1/ctar2. here, found bs69 directly
interacted lmp1/ctar1 domain regulated lmp1/ctar1-mediated nf-kappab
activation subsequent il-6 production. regarding mechanisms involved, 
found bs69 directly interacted traf3, negative regulator nf-kappab
activation. furthermore, small-interfering rna-mediated knockdown experiments
revealed traf3 involved bs69-mediated suppression of
lmp1/ctar1-induced nf-kappab activation.
