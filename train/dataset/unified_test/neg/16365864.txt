a case series pediatric primary central nervous system lymphoma: favorable
outcome without cranial irradiation.

abla o(1), sandlund jt, sung l, brock p, corbett r, kirov i, griffin tc, blaser
s, weitzman s.

author information: 
(1)division hematology/oncology, hospital sick children, toronto,
ontario, canada. oussama.abla@sickkids.ca

purpose: describe outcome childhood primary central nervous system
lymphoma (pcnsl) treated chemotherapy alone chemotherapy plus
cranial radiotherapy (crt).
methods: retrospective chart review children pcnsl six tertiary care
pediatric centers.
results: eight immunocompetent four immunocompromised children included.
ten children received chemotherapy alone without crt, receiving
high-dose methotrexate high-dose cytarabine. five year event-free survival
(efs) group 70.0 +/- 14.5%. two children received chemotherapy plus
crt; one relapsed died alive remission. three children 
died, including two relapsed disease. child human
immunodeficiency virus infection died opportunistic infection in
remission following chemotherapy alone.
conclusions: children pcnsl achieve long-term remissions with
chemotherapy alone without crt. multi-center prospective studies needed to
confirm refute results larger number patients.
