a solitary extraskeletal osteochondroma diagnosed 6-year-old, castrated 
male burmese cat, positive feline leukaemia virus (felv). cat presented
with rapidly growing, solid, non-painful mass craniolateral aspect of
the left elbow. radiographs revealed oval, well circumscribed 2.0 cm × 1.5 cm 
× 1.5 cm mineralised mass separated underlying bone. surgical excisional
biopsy confirmed diagnosis. feline extraskeletal osteochondromas benign
tumours frequently seen felv-positive cats transform into
osteosarcomas chondrosarcomas. radiographically, cannot distinguished 
from parosteal extraskeletal osteosarcoma.
