background: pupillary disorders following varicella infections rare few
cases reported partially pharmacologically tested.
materials methods: 5-year-old boy presented left-sided dilated and
unreactive pupil, 3 months suffered chickenpox the
course acute disease developed anterior uveitis treated with
local prednisone mydriatics. pupil remained dilated despite discontinued 
medication, light reaction absent accommodation impaired. pilocarpine
supersensitivity indication lesion sphincter muscle and
reduced response cholinesterase inhibitors (physostigmine) pointed towards a
postganglionic lesion.
conclusions: probable varicella virus infection short ciliary
nerves ciliary ganglion caused anterior uveitis postganglionic
parasympathetic nerve lesion, clinically presenting tonic pupil. according
to cases previously reported irreversible nerve lesion assumed.
