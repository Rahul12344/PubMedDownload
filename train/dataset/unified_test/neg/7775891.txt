
1. j egypt public health assoc. 1994;69(1-2):115-28.

study knowledge, attitude egyptian health care workers towards
occupational hiv infection.

faris r(1), shouman a.

author information: 
(1)faculty medicine, shams university.

acquired immuno-deficiency syndrome (aids) reported first time in
egypt 1986. march 1993, 60 cases notified ministry health
and 359 detected infected human immunodeficiency virus (hiv).
most cases acquired infection abroad blood, blood products sexual 
contact. number cases continuously increasing. fear ignorance about
hiv infection causes panic among health care workers (hcws) whenever case is
discovered needs medical care. assessed knowledge attitude 346 
hcws towards probability occupational hiv infection using self
administered questionnaire. sample included physicians, surgeons, nurses,
laboratory technicians ambulance workers. 72.8% hcws believed they
are "great" danger acquiring aids occupational exposure, whereas
0.6% thought danger. 67% hcws wrong ideas about
transmission hiv (toilet seats, droplet infection, touching patients). 83.5%
mentioned aids patients isolated quarantine. main source of
information aids television, radio ordinary press. 44.8% got
their information textbooks scientific literature. 95.4% felt need
for information infection disease. results show urgent need
to start program education hcws concerning risk occupational hiv
infection measures prevention.
