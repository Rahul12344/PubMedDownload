rare clinical presentation tuberculous meningitis: case report.

tan jl(1), nordin s(2), besari am(2).

author information: 
(1)school medical sciences universiti sains malaysia, health campus, 16150
kubang kerian, kelantan, malaysia.
(2)department medicine, universiti sains malaysia, 16150 kubang kerian,
kelantan, malaysia.

introduction: tuberculosis second leading cause death the
category infectious diseases, human immunodeficiency virus (hiv).
tuberculous meningitis (tbm) constitutes 5% extrapulmonary disease
worldwide. report describes case tuberculous meningitis rare
presentation 28-year-old woman, treated based collection her
social background, clinical findings multiplex pcr tuberculosis.
case presentation: 28-year-old malay woman significant medical history 
presented husm one month history fever, two weeks history
of generalised limbs weakness one week history dysphagia. reported
to experienced visual hallucination significant weight loss. her
laboratory result significant leukocytosis, elevated esr and
hypernatremia. non-enhanced contrast ct scan brain showed severe
bilateral frontal cerebral atrophy. cerebral spinal fluid (csf) multiplex pcr
for mycobacterium tuberculosis complex positive. promptly started 
anti-tb regime combined dexamethasone. subsequent follow-up showed
significant improvement.
conclusion: rare clinical manifestation tuberculous meningitis 
demonstrates importance recognising initiating treatment early 
reduce disabilities improve clinical outcome.
