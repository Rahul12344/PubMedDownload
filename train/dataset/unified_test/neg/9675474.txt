we describe child congenital hypogammaglobulinemia diagnosed at
13 months age. 4 years old, gait disturbances began. main
neurological manifestations progressive spastic tetraparesis and
intellectual speech deterioration. infectious agent identified. a
magnetic resonance imaging scan central nervous system revealed
periventricular demyelinating areas frontal, temporal, parietal lobes 
with cortical atrophy. stereotactic brain biopsy confirmed diagnosis of
progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy caused jc virus. treated
with intravenous intraventricular cytarabine interferon-alpha, 
was clinical improvement. emphasize need brain biopsy soon a
neurological complication suspected patients congenital
hypogammaglobulinemia cerebrospinal cultures polymerase chain
reaction analyses negative.
