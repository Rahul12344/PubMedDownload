we report case extranodal nk/t-cell lymphoma, nasal type, exclusive
cerebral localization patient aids. patient presented with
neurological alterations, fever convulsions, initial presumptive
diagnosis opportunistic brain infection. mri showed left parietal
necrotic lesion stereotactic brain biopsy performed pathological,
microbiological molecular studies. histological sections showed an
angiocentric angiodestructive growth pattern immunophenotype 
tumor cd56+, cd45+, cd3+ (cytoplasmic), granzyme b+ perforin+. the
microbiological studies bacterial, fungi, micobacteria, toxoplasma gondii
and cryptococcus determination negatives. pcr study primer specific
for ebv viral genome bam-hi-w system positive. also, rearrangement study
showed t-cell gene rearrangement monoclonal appearance. diagnosis of
extranodal nk/t-cell lymphoma made patient died days later.
this case represents rare example nk/t-cell lymphoma brain 
patient aids. diagnosis kind lymphomas requires a
multimodality approach correlating clinical, morphological, immunophenotypic 
molecular data.
