introduction: adequate level personal protective equipment (ppe) is
necessary treating patients highly infectious diseases those
contaminated hazardous substances.
methods: following national institute health research's research centre
(london, united kingdom) approval, authors study conducted survey
of specialist registrars' knowledge respiratory skin protection
requirements needed resuscitation scenario advanced life support.
participant responses compared uk national recommendations a
previous survey 2009.
results: total 98 specialist registrars (in anesthesiology, n=51; in
emergency medicine (em), n=21; intensive care medicine (icm) n=26)
completed hand-delivered surveys. best knowledge ppe requirements (76%)
was found severe acute respiratory syndrome (sars), less knowledge about
ppe requirements anthrax, plague, ebola virus disease (evd), smallpox
(60%). results show limited knowledge ppe requirements (20%-30%) for
various chemical warfare agents. personal protective equipment knowledge
regarding treatment sarin-contaminated casualties over-rated 80%, and
for patients evd, over-rated 67% participants.
conclusion: results tested cohort indicate current knowledge
regarding ppe chemical warfare agents remains limited.
