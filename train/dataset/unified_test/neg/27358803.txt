setting: health facilities providing tuberculosis (tb) care swaziland.
objective: describe impact human immunodeficiency virus (hiv)
interventions trend tb treatment outcomes 2010-2013 in
swaziland; describe evolution tb case notification, uptake of
hiv testing, antiretroviral therapy (art) cotrimoxazole preventive therapy
(cpt), proportion tb-hiv co-infected patients adverse treatment
outcomes, including mortality, loss follow-up treatment failure.
design: retrospective descriptive study using aggregated national tb programme 
data.
results: 2010 2013, tb case notifications swaziland decreased by
40%, hiv testing increased 86% 96%, cpt uptake increased 93% 99% 
and art uptake among tb patients increased 35% 75%. tb-hiv
co-infection rate remained around 70% proportion tb-hiv cases with
adverse outcomes decreased 36% 30%. mortality remained high, 14-16%,
over study period, anti-tuberculosis treatment failure rates stable 
over time (<5%).
conclusion: despite high cpt art uptake tb-hiv patients, mortality
remained high. studies required better define high-risk patient
groups, understand reasons death design appropriate interventions.
