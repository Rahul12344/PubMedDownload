background: delivery comprehensive care persons human
immunodeficiency virus (hiv) infection rural low prevalence settings
presents many challenges. developed evaluated telehealth collaborative
care (tcc) program persons hiv rural area.
objective: determine feasibility tcc, identify factors influencing 
implementation rural settings.
design: mixed methods evaluation quality improvement program with
pre-measures post-measures.
patients: veterans hiv infection iowa illinois.
intervention: tcc integrated hiv specialty care delivered clinical video
telehealth, primary care delivered generalist providers, seven
community based outpatient clinics (cbocs) serving rural areas. principles
guiding tcc design were: 1) clear delineation specialty primary care
clinic roles co-managed care; 2) creation processes improve care
coordination specialty primary care teams; 3) use patient
registry population management across sites.
measures: veterans affairs (va) healthcare system performance measures care
for hiv infection common comorbidities, patient travel time obtain care,
and patient satisfaction. qualitative evaluation involved semi-structured
telephone interviews patients.
key results: thirty 32 eligible patients chose tcc traveling hiv
clinic care. among 24 patients tcc june 2011-may 2012
evaluation period, median age 54 (range, 40-79), (96 %) men, and
median cd4 count 707 cells/cm(3) (range, 233-1307). va performance measures
were met > 90 % tcc patients. median yearly travel time decreased from
320 min per patient prior tcc 170 min tcc (p < 0.001). interview
themes included: 1) overcoming privacy concerns care local primary care
clinics; 2) tradeoffs access, continuity, care coordination; 3)
the role specialist involvement collaborative care.
discussion: telehealth collaborative care feasible approach providing
accessible comprehensive care persons hiv rural settings.
