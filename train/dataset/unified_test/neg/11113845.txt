
1. j pediatr. 2000 dec;137(6):865-70.

rates hospitalization respiratory syncytial virus infection among children
in medicaid.

boyce tg(1), mellen bg, mitchel ef jr, wright pf, griffin mr.

author information: 
(1)department pediatric adolescent medicine, mayo clinic mayo medical 
school, rochester, minnesota 55905, usa.

objective: determine rates hospitalization associated respiratory
syncytial virus (rsv) infection among children without specific medical 
conditions.
study design: retrospective cohort study children <3 years old enrolled in
the tennessee medicaid program july 1989 june 1993 (248,652
child-years).
results: first year life, estimated number rsv
hospitalizations per 1000 children 388 bronchopulmonary
dysplasia, 92 congenital heart disease, 70 children born < 
or = 28 weeks' gestation, 66 born 29 <33 weeks, 57 born
at 33 <36 weeks, 30 children born term underlying medical
condition. second year life, children bronchopulmonary dysplasia
had estimated 73 rsv hospitalizations per 1000 children, whereas with
congenital heart disease 18 prematurity 16 per 1000. overall, 
53% rsv hospitalizations occurred healthy children born term.
conclusions: children bronchopulmonary dysplasia high rates rsv
hospitalization 24 months age. contrast, first year of
life, children congenital heart disease prematurity rates higher 
than children low risk <12 months old.
