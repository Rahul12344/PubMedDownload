the dna genome hepatitis b virus (hbv) replicates via reverse transcription
within capsids following encapsidation rna template, pregenomic rna
(pgrna). previously demonstrated 5' cap proximity stem-loop
structure (epsilon epsilon), encapsidation signal, critically important 
for encapsidation pgrna (j. k. jeong, g. s. yoon, w. s. ryu, j.
virol. 74:5502-5508, 2000). therefore, speculated viral polymerase
(pol), bound 5' epsilon stem-loop structure, could recognize cap
via interaction eif4e, eukaryotic translation initiation factor. our
data showed direct interaction hbv pol eif4e, measured by
coimmunoprecipitation. further, demonstrated eif4e interacts the
pol-epsilon ribonucleoprotein complex (rnp) rather pol alone, resulting in
eif4e-pol-epsilon rnp complex formation. addition, asked whether eif4e
remains engaged pol-epsilon rnp complex nucleocapsid assembly.
density gradient analysis revealed eif4e indeed incorporated into
nucleocapsids. great importance uncover whether incorporated
eif4e contributes viral reverse transcription steps hbv life
cycle.
