While sensitivity to odours varies from one individual to another, bad smells can
instinctively and uncontrollably induce nausea and revulsion. Different treatment
strategies can be implemented. They consist in neutralising the odours, adding
more pleasant smells and/or targeting the bacteria. The management of odours
remains a complex problem without any universal or single solution. Odour control
must not be used as a replacement for adapted hygiene and wound care.
