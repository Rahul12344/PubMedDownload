Moral distress amid COVID-19: A frontline emergency nurse's perspective.

Virani SB(1).

Author information: 
(1)Salima Bano Virani is an ED nurse at St. Michaels Hospital in Toronto, Canada,
and a sessional faculty member in the School of Community and Health Studies at
Centennial College in Ontario.

ABSTRACT: ED nurses are at high risk for developing moral distress during the
COVID-19 pandemic. Predisposing factors include limited resources, inadequate
staffing, PPE shortages, and caring for vulnerable populations. This article
explores personal and organizational strategies to help nurses cope with moral
distress.
