The family Belpaoviridae comprises metazoan-infecting reverse-transcribing
viruses with long terminal repeats, commonly known as Bel/Pao LTR
retrotransposons. These viruses share evolutionary history and genes involved in 
genome replication and virion formation with reverse-transcribing viruses of the 
families Metaviridae, Pseudoviridae, Retroviridae and Caulimoviridae. These five 
families form the order Ortervirales. This is a summary of the ICTV Report on the
family Belpaoviridae, which is available at ictv.global/report/belpaoviridae.
