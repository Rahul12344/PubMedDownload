Nef-specific CD107a+ CD4+ T-cell responses in a rhesus macaque (Macaca mulatta)
showing partial simian immunodeficiency virus control following passive
neutralizing antibody infusion.

Hau TTT(1)(2), Kanno Y(1)(3), Nishizawa M(1), Nomura T(1), Matano T(1)(2)(3),
Yamamoto H(1)(4).

Author information:

Acute-phase neutralizing antibody (NAb) passive immunization in simian
immunodeficiency virus (SIV)-infected rhesus macaques (Macaca mulatta) can confer
stringent viremia control with T-cell augmentation. In one NAb-infused SIV
partial controller, we identify chronic-phase Nef-specific CD107a+ CD4+ T-cell
response maintenance, implicating that NAb infusion modulates long-term T-cell
responses even within viremic control.
