Generating patient-specific stem cells representing the onset of development has 
become possible since the discovery of somatic cell reprogramming into induced
pluripotent stem cells. However, human pluripotent stem cells are generally
cultured in a primed pluripotent state: they are poised for differentiation and
represent a stage of development corresponding to post-implantation epiblast.
Here, we describe a protocol to reprogram human fibroblasts into naive
pluripotent stem cells by overexpressing the transcription factors OCT4, SOX2,
KLF4, and c-MYC using Sendai viruses. The resulting cells represent an earlier
stage of development that corresponds to pre-implantation epiblast. We also
discuss validation methods for human naive pluripotent stem cells.
