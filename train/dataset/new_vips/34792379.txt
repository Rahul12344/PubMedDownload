An "Uncharacterized" Australian Virus Is the Earliest Known Example of Ross River
Virus with Changes in the nsP3 Protein Associated with the Explosive Outbreak of 
Ross River Virus Infection in the Pacific Region from 1979 to 1980.

Aaskov JG(1)(2), Graham M(1)(3), Liu W(1).

Author information: 
(1)Australian Defence Force Malaria and Infectious Disease Institute, Enoggera,
Australia.
(2)Queensland University of Technology, Brisbane, Australia.
(3)Queensland Institute of Medical Research-Berghofer Medical Research Institute,
Brisbane, Australia.

Ross River virus recovered from a South Australian patient during an outbreak of 
epidemic polyarthritis in 1971 is the earliest known genome sequence with the
duplicated 12-amino-acid motif in the nsP3 protein that was found in strains
responsible for the outbreak of epidemic polyarthritis in the Pacific region from
1979 to 1980.
