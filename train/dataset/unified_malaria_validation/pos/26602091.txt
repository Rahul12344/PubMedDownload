to identify cytokine signatures differentiating severe malaria patients mod,
cerebral malaria (cm), cerebral malaria mod (cm-mod) india. have
previously shown two cytokines clusters differentiated cm mild malaria 
in maharashtra. hence, also aimed determine cytokines could
discriminate malaria subphenotypes odisha.
methods: p. falciparum malaria patients scb medical college cuttack in
the odisha state india enrolled along three sets controls:
healthy individuals, patients sepsis encephalitis (n = 222). we
determined plasma concentrations pro- anti-inflammatory cytokines and
chemokines individuals using multiplex assay. used ensemble 
of statistical analytical methods ascertain whether particular sets of
cytokines/chemokines predictors severity signatures disease
category.
results: 26 cytokines/chemokines tested, 19 increased significantly during
malaria clearly distinguished malaria patients controls, well as
sepsis encephalitis patients. high amounts il-17, ip-10, il-10
predicted mod, decreased il-17 mip-1α segregated cm-mod mod, and
increased il-12p40 differentiated cm cm-mod. severe malaria patients
with arf exhibited high levels il-17.
conclusion: report distinct differences cytokine production correlating
with malarial disease severity odisha maharashtra populations india. we
show cm, cm-mod mod clearly distinct malaria-associated pathologies.
high amounts il-17, ip-10, il-10 predictors mod; decreased il-17
and mip-1α separated cm-mod mod; increased il-12p40 differentiated cm
from cm-mod. data also suggest il-17 pathway may contribute malaria
pathogenesis via different regulatory mechanisms may represent interesting
