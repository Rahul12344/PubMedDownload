background objectives: plasmodium falciparum infection pregnancy is
characterised placental inefficiency caused infected erythrocyte
sequestration. reduced placental efficiency leads placental intrauterine
adaptation sustaining fetal growth, reflected changes the
expression placental genes involved intrauterine growth regulation.
therefore, aimed determine whether placental weight ratio, indicator
of placental efficiency, affects placental expression components of
the insulin-like growth factor axis leptin signalling pathway p.
falciparum-infected pregnant women.
methods study design: malaria case-only analysis 50 p.
falciparum-infected pregnant women timika, papua, indonesia, conducted.
the placental mrna expression insulin-like growth factor-i, insulin-like
growth factor binding protein-1, leptin, long short isoforms the
leptin receptor measured quantitative realtime pcr.
results: placental weight ratio exerted positive effect placental
mrna expression insulin- like growth factor-i (coefficient=6.10, p=0.002) 
the long isoform leptin receptor (coefficient= 4.73, p=0.015) in
malaria-infected pregnant women without fever chill symptoms.
conclusion: results indicate placental adaptive responses caused by
adverse intrauterine conditions p. falciparuminfected pregnant women vary
depending presence absence fever chill symptoms.
