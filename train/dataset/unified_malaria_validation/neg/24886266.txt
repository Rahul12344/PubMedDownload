background: plasmodium knowlesi simian parasite recognized 
the fifth species causing human malaria. naturally-acquired p. knowlesi infection
is widespread among human populations southeast asia. aim this
epidemiological study determine incidence distribution malaria 
parasites, particular focus human p. knowlesi infection malaysia.
methods: total 457 microscopically confirmed, malaria-positive blood samples
were collected 22 state main district hospitals malaysia between
september 2012 december 2013. nested pcr assay targeting 18s rrna gene
was used determine infecting plasmodium species.
results: total 453 samples positive plasmodium species using
nested pcr assay. plasmodium knowlesi identified 256 (56.5%) samples,
followed 133 (29.4%) cases plasmodium vivax, 49 (10.8%) cases plasmodium
falciparum, two (0.4%) cases plasmodium ovale one (0.2%) case of
plasmodium malariae. twelve mixed infections detected, including p.
knowlesi/p. vivax (n = 10), p. knowlesi/p. falciparum (n = 1), p.
falciparum/p. vivax (n = 1). notably, p. knowlesi (included mixed infections
involving p. knowlesi (p. knowlesi/p. vivax p. knowlesi /p. falciparum))
showed highest proportion sabah (84/115 cases, prevalence 73.0%),
sarawak (83/120, 69.2%), kelantan (42/56, 75.0%), pahang (24/25, 96.0%), johor
(7/9, 77.8%), terengganu (4/5, 80.0%,). contrast, rates p. knowlesi
infection selangor negeri sembilan found 16.2% (18/111 cases)
and 50.0% (5/10 cases), respectively. sample p. knowlesi obtained from
kuala lumpur, melaka, perak, pulau pinang, perlis study period,
while microscopically-positive sample kedah negative pcr.
conclusion: addition sabah sarawak, known high
prevalence p. knowlesi infection, findings study highlight the
widespread distribution p. knowlesi many peninsular malaysia states.
