background: malaria one serious vector-borne diseases the
world. vector control important measure malaria prevention and
elimination. however, strategy threat disease vectors are
developing resistance insecticides. therefore, important monitor
mechanisms responsible insecticide resistance. study, presence of
g119s mutation acetyl cholinesterase-encoding gene (ace-1) was
investigated nine anopheles sinensis populations sampled across guangxi zhuang
autonomous region china.
methods: pcr-rflp (polymerase chain reaction-restriction fragment length
polymorphism) method used genotype individual adult an. sinensis. 
direct sequencing pcr products performed verify accuracy of
pcr-rflp genotyping result. population genetics analysis conducted using
genepop programme.
results: frequencies susceptible homozygotes, heterozygotes resistant 
homozygotes nine populations ranged 0-0.296, 0.143-0.500 and
0.333-0.857, respectively. overall, high frequency (0.519-0.929) mutant 119s
allele observed genotype frequency ace-1 gene an. sinensis 
was hardy-weinberg equilibrium nine examined populations.
conclusion: g119s mutation become fixed widespread an. sinensis
field populations guangxi, china. findings useful helping design 
strategies an. sinensis control.
