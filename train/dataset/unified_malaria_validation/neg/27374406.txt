plasmodium falciparum parasites merozoite stage invade human erythrocytes 
and cause malaria. invasion requires multiple interactions merozoite
ligands erythrocyte receptors. p. falciparum reticulocyte binding homolog 5
(pfrh5) forms complex pfrh5-interacting protein (pfripr) and
cysteine-rich protective antigen (cyrpa) binds erythrocytes via host
receptor basigin. however, specific role pfripr cyrpa play during
invasion unclear. using p. falciparum lines conditionally expressing pfripr
and cyrpa, show loss pfripr cyrpa function blocks growth due the
inability merozoites invade erythrocytes. super-resolution microscopy
revealed pfripr, cyrpa, pfrh5 colocalize junction between
merozoites erythrocytes invasion. pfripr, cyrpa, and
pfripr/cyrpa/pfrh5-basigin complex required triggering ca(2+) release 
and establishing tight junction. together, results establish the
pfrh5/pfripr/cyrpa complex essential sequential molecular events
leading parasite invasion human erythrocytes.
