we describe type species novel genus microsporidian parasite,
pseudokabatana alburnus n. gen. n. sp., infecting liver topmouth culter,
culter alburnus basilewsky, 1855, lake poyang xingzi county, jiangxi
province, china. parasite elicits formation spherical xenomas to
1.2 mm diameter containing observed life stages early merogonal
plasmodia mature spores contained within cytoplasm host hepatocytes.
merogonal plasmodia existed direct contact host cytoplasm and
contained 20 visible nuclei. plasmotomy multinucleate plasmodium led
to formation uninucleate cells nucleus underwent division
to form bi-nucleate presporonts, sporonts (defined cells thickened
endospore) eventually sporoblasts (containing pre-cursors spore
extrusion apparatus). mature spores pyriform monokaryotic, measuring
2.3 ± 0.19 μm long 1.3 ± 0.10 μm wide. spores possessed bipartite
polaroplast 5-6 coils polar filament, single rank. obtained
partial ssu rrna gene sequence, 1383 bp length, match of
microsporidia available genbank. ssu rdna-based phylogenetic analysis
indicated new taxon branching kabatana rondoni, parasite infecting the
skeletal muscle gymnorhamphichthys rondoni amazon river. due to
different host tissue tropism, novel taxon fit diagnostic
criteria genus kabatana. further, based ssu rdna-inferred phylogenetic
analyses, different ultrastructural features developmental stages, and
ecological considerations, new genus pseudokabatana type species
pseudokabatana alburnus n. sp. erected parasite topmouth culter.
