plasmodium falciparum malaria imposes serious public health concern throughout 
the tropics. although genetic tools principally important fully
investigate malaria parasites, currently available forward reverse tools 
fairly limited. expected parasites high mutation rate can
readily acquire novel phenotypes/traits; however, remain untapped tool
for malaria biology. here, generated mutator malaria parasite (hereinafter
called 'malaria mutator'), using site-directed mutagenesis gene
transfection techniques. mutator plasmodium berghei line defective
proofreading 3' → 5' exonuclease activity dna polymerase δ (referred as
pbmut) control p. berghei line wild-type dna polymerase δ (referred to
as pbctl) maintained weekly passage ddy mice 122 weeks.
high-throughput genome sequencing analysis revealed two pbmut lines had
175-178 mutations 86- 90-fold higher mutation rate pbctl 
line. pbmut, pbctl, parent strain, pbwt, showed similar course of
infection. interestingly, pbmut lost ability form gametocytes during
serial passages. believe malaria mutator system could provide novel
and useful tool investigate malaria biology.
