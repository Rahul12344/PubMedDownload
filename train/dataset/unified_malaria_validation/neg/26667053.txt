background: plasmodium falciparum malaria significant public health problem 
in comoros, artemisinin combination therapy (act) remains first choice
for treating acute uncomplicated p. falciparum. emergence spread of
artemisinin-resistant p. falciparum southeast asia, associated mutations 
in k13-propeller gene, poses potential threat act efficacy. detection of
mutations p. falciparum k13-propeller gene may provide first-hand
information changes parasite susceptibility artemisinin. objective
of study determinate prevalence mutant k13-propeller gene among
the p. falciparum isolates collected grande comore island, union comoros,
where act use since 2004.
methods: total 207 p. falciparum clinical isolates collected the
island march 2006 october 2007 (n = 118) march 2013 december
2014 (n = 89). isolates analysed single nucleotide polymorphisms
(snps) haplotypes k13-propeller gene using nested pcr dna
sequencing.
results: three 2006-2007 samples carried snps k13-propeller gene, one
having synonymous (g538g) two non-synonymous (s477y and
d584e) substitutions leading two mutated haplotypes (2.2%, 2/95). three
synonymous mutations (r471r, y500y, g538g) (5.9%, 5/85) 7 non-synonymous 
substitutions (21.2%, 18/85) nine mutated haplotypes (18.8%, 16/85) were
found isolates 2013 2014. however, none polymorphisms
associated artemisinin-resistance southeast asia detected of
the parasites examined.
conclusion: study showed increased k13-propeller gene diversity among p.
falciparum populations island course 8 years (2006-2014).
nevertheless, none polymorphisms known associated artemisinin
resistance asia detected parasite populations examined. data
suggest p. falciparum populations grande comore still effectively
susceptible artemisinin. results provide insights p. falciparum
populations regarding mutations gene associated artemisinin
resistance useful developing updating anti-malarial guidance 
in comoros.
