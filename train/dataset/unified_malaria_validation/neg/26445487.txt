voltage-gated sodium channel gene para, including l1014f kdr mutation
associated insensitivity pyrethroid insecticides. carriage l1014f kdr 
is also correlated higher susceptibility infection plasmodium
falciparum. however, genetic mechanism causative gene(s) underlying the
parasite susceptibility phenotype known.
methods: mosquitoes wild burkina faso population challenged by
feeding natural p. falciparum gametocytes. oocyst infection phenotypes were
determined tested association snp genotypes. candidate genes in
the detected locus prioritized rnai-mediated gene silencing used 
functionally test gene effects p. falciparum susceptibility.
results: genetic locus, pfin6, identified influences infection levels 
of p. falciparum mosquitoes. locus segregates ~3 mb haplotype
carrying 65 predicted genes including para gene. haplotype carrying the
kdr allele para linked increased parasite infection prevalence, many
single nucleotide polymorphisms haplotype also equally linked the
infection phenotype. candidate genes haplotype prioritized and
functionally tested. silencing para influence p. falciparum infection,
while silencing predicted immune gene, serine protease clipc9, allowed
development significantly increased parasite numbers.
conclusions: genetic variation influencing plasmodium infection wild anopheles
is linked natural ~3 megabase haplotype chromosome 2l carries the
kdr allele para gene. evidence suggests para gene function not
directly influence parasite susceptibility, association kdr with
infection may due tight linkage kdr gene(s) haplotype.

influence para.
