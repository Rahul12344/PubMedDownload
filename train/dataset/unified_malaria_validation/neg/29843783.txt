background: burkina faso, malaria remains overall leading cause of
morbidity mortality accounting 35.12% consultations, 40.83% of
hospitalizations 37.5% deaths. genotyping malaria parasite populations 
remains important tool determine types number parasite clones in
an infection. present study aimed evaluate merozoite surface protein 1
(msp1) merozoite surface protein 2 (msp2) genetic diversity allele
frequencies bobo-dioulasso, burkina faso.
method: dried blood spots (dbs) collected baseline patients with
uncomplicated malaria urban health centers bobo-dioulasso. parasite dna was
extracted using chelex-100 species identified using nested pcr.
plamodium falciparum msp1 msp2 genes amplified nested polymerase
chain reaction (pcr) pcr products analyzed electrophoresis 2.5% 
agarose gel. alleles categorized according molecular weight.
results: total 228 blood samples analyzed 227 (99.9%) were
confirmed p. falciparum-positive one sample classified mixed infection 
for p. malaria p. falciparum. msp1, k1 allelic family predominant 
with 77.4% (162/209) followed respectively mad20 allelic family 41.3%
and r033 allelic family 36%. msp2, 3d7 allelic family most
frequently detected 93.1 % compared fc27 41.3%. twenty-one different
alleles observed msp1 9 alleles k1, 8 alleles mad20 4
alleles r033. msp2, 25 individual alleles detected 10 alleles
for fc27 15 alleles 3d7. mean multiplicity falciparum infection
was 1.95 respectively 1.8 (1.76-1.83) 2.1 (2.03-2.16) msp1 msp2 
(p = 0.01).
conclusions: study showed high genetic diversity allelic frequencies of
msp1 msp2 plasmodium falciparum isolates symptomatic malaria patients
in bobo-dioulasso.
