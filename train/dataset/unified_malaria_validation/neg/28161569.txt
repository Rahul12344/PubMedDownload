background: evidence suggests pfkelch13 mutations confer
artemisinin resistance falciparum malaria multiple independent origins
across greater mekong subregion, motivated regional malaria
elimination agenda. aimed use molecular genotyping assess antimalarial
drug resistance selection spread greater mekong subregion.
methods: observational study, tested plasmodium falciparum isolates
from myanmar, northeastern thailand, southern laos, western cambodia for
pfkelch13 mutations pfplasmepsin2 gene amplification (indicating
piperaquine resistance). collected blood spots patients microscopy
or rapid test confirmed uncomplicated falciparum malaria. used microsatellite 
genotyping assess genetic relatedness.
findings: part studies epidemiology artemisinin-resistant malaria
between jan 1, 2008, dec 31, 2015, collected 434 isolates. 2014-15, a
single long pfkelch13 c580y haplotype (-50 +31·5 kb) lineage, emerged in
western cambodia 2008, detected 65 88 isolates northeastern
thailand, 86 111 isolates southern laos, 14 14 isolates from
western cambodia, signifying hard transnational selective sweep. pfplasmepsin2 
amplification occurred within lineage, 2015 closely
related parasites found ten 14 isolates cambodia 15 15
isolates northeastern thailand. c580y mutated parasites myanmar a
different genetic origin.
interpretation: results suggest dominant artemisinin-resistant p
falciparum c580y lineage probably arose western cambodia spread to
thailand laos, outcompeting parasites acquiring piperaquine
resistance. emergence spread fit artemisinin-resistant p falciparum
parasite lineages, acquire partner drug resistance across greater 
mekong subregion, threatens regional malaria control elimination goals.
elimination falciparum malaria region accelerated while
available antimalarial drugs still remain effective.
