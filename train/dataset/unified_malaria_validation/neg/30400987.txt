background: recently, anopheles considered naturally unable to
host wolbachia, intracellular bacterium regarded potential biological
control tool. detection field populations anopheles gambiae sensu
lato, suggests may also present many anopheline species than
previously thought.
results: here, reported first discovery natural wolbachia infections in
anopheles funestus populations senegal, second main malaria vector in
africa. molecular phylogeny analysis based 16s rrna gene revealed least
two wolbachia genotypes named wanfu-a wanfu-b, according 
close relatedness b supergroups. furthermore, wanfu genotypes
displayed high proximity wanga sequences previously described an.
gambiae complex, nucleotide differences. however, low
prevalence infection, together difficulties encountered for
detection, whatever method used, highlights need develop effective 
sensitive wolbachia screening method dedicated anopheline.
conclusions: discovery natural wolbachia infection an. funestus,
another major malaria vector, may overcome main limitation using a
wolbachia-based approach control malaria population suppression and/or
replacement.
