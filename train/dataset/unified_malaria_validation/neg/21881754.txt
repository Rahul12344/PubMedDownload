analysis genetic variability pvmsp-3α among plasmodium vivax in
brazilian field isolates.

ribeiro rs(1), ladeira l, rezende am, fontes cj, carvalho lh, brito cf.

author information: 
(1)laboratório de malária, instituto de pesquisa rené rachou-fiocruz, av. augusto
de lima 1715, 30190-002 belo horizonte, mg, brasil.

reliable molecular markers essential better understanding the
molecular epidemiology plasmodium vivax, neglected human malaria
parasite. aim study analyze genetic diversity p. vivax 
isolates brazilian amazon using polymerase chain reaction-restriction
fragment length polymorphism (pcr-rflp) analysis highly polymorphic
merozoite surface protein-3alpha (pvmsp-3α) gene. accomplish this, 60 isolates
of p. vivax different endemic areas brazilian amazon collected. 
the pvmsp-3α gene amplified nested-pcr. three major types pvmsp-3α 
locus detected different frequencies: type (68%), b (15%) c (17%). 
a single sample showed two pcr fragments, corresponded infection with
types c. pcr-rflp analysis using hhai restriction enzyme 52
isolates clearly identified 11 haplotypes, eight type a, two
from type b one type c. seven isolates show clear
pattern using pcr-rflp. result might due multiple clone infections.
this study showed high diversity pvmsp-3α gene among p. vivax isolates
from brazilian amazon, also indicated detection performance of
pcr-rflp pvmsp-3α gene may sufficient detect multiple clone
infections.
