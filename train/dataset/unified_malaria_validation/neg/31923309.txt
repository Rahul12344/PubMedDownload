background: genome candida albicans displays significant polymorphism.
point mutations genes involved resistance antifungals may either confer 
phenotypic resistance devoid phenotypic consequences.
objectives: catalogue polymorphisms azole echinocandin resistance genes
occurring susceptible strains order rapidly pinpoint relevant mutations 
in resistant strains.
methods: genome sequences 151 unrelated c. albicans strains susceptible to
fluconazole caspofungin used create catalogue non-synonymous
polymorphisms genes involved resistance azoles (erg11, tac1, mrr1 and
upc2) echinocandins (fks1). potential catalogue reveal putative
resistance mutations tested 10 azole-resistant isolates, including 1
intermediate caspofungin. selected mutations analysed mutagenesis
experiments mutational prediction effect.
results: susceptible strains, identified 126 amino acid substitutions
constituting catalogue phenotypically neutral polymorphisms. excluding 
these neutral substitutions, identified 22 additional substitutions 10 
resistant strains. among substitutions, 10 already associated with
resistance. remaining 12 tac1p (n = 6), upc2p (n = 2) erg11p
(n = 4). four six homozygous substitutions tac1p (h263y, a790v,
h839y p971s) conferred increases azole mics, effects were
observed upc2p. additionally, two homozygous substitutions (y64h and
p236s) predicted conformation effect erg11p.
conclusions: establishing catalogue neutral polymorphisms occurring in
genes involved resistance antifungal drugs, provide useful resource
for rapid identification mutations possibly responsible phenotypic
resistance c. albicans.
