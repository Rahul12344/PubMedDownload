background: plasmodium falciparum apical membrane antigen-1 (pfama1) is
considered ideal vaccine candidate malaria control due high
level immunogenicity essential role parasite survival. among three 
domains pfama1 protein, hyper-variable region (hvr) domain most
immunogenic. present study conducted evaluate extent genetic
diversity across hvr domain pfama1 gene p. falciparum isolates from
hazara division pakistan.
methods: hvr domain pfama1 amplified sequenced 20 p.
falciparum positive cases hazara division pakistan. sequences were
analysed context global population data p. falciparum nine malaria 
endemic countries. dna sequence reads quality assessment, reads assembling,
sequences alignment/phylogenetic population genetic analyses performed
using staden, lasergene v. 7.1, mega7 dnasp v.5 software packages
respectively.
results: total 14 mutations found pakistani isolates 12 parsimony
informative sites. comparison global isolates, novel non-synonymous
mutation (y240f) found specifically single pakistani sample 5%
frequency. less number mutations, haplotypes, recombination low
pairwise nucleotide differences revealed tightly linked uniform genetic structure
with low genetic diversity hvr domain pfama1 among p. falciparum isolates
from hazara region pakistan. uniform genetic structure may shaped
across pakistani p. falciparum isolates bottleneck natural selection
events.
conclusion: pakistani p. falciparum isolates found maintain a
distinct genetic pattern hvr pfama1 extent genetic relationship
with geographically close myanmar indian samples. however, exact pattern 
of gene flow demographic events may infer whole genome sequence data
with large sample size p. falciparum collected broad area pakistan.
