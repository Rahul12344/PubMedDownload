zinc-finger antiviral protein inhibits hiv-1 infection selectively targeting
multiply spliced viral mrnas degradation.

zhu y(1), chen g, lv f, wang x, ji x, xu y, sun j, wu l, zheng yt, gao g.

author information: 
(1)key laboratory infection immunity, institute biophysics, chinese
academy sciences, beijing 100101, china.

the zinc-finger antiviral protein (zap) originally identified host
factor inhibits replication moloney murine leukemia virus. we
report zap inhibits hiv-1 infection promoting degradation specific
viral mrnas. overexpression zap rendered cells resistant hiv-1 infection in
a zap expression level-dependent manner, whereas depletion endogenous zap
enhanced hiv-1 infection. human rat zap inhibited propagation of
replication-competent hiv-1. zap specifically targeted multiply spliced but
not unspliced singly spliced hiv-1 mrnas degradation. provide evidence 
indicating zap selectively recruits cellular poly(a)-specific ribonuclease
(parn) shorten poly(a) tail target viral mrna recruits rna
exosome degrade rna body 3' end. addition, zap recruits
cellular decapping complex cofactor rna helicase p72 initiate
degradation target viral mrna 5' end. depletion 
mrna degradation enzymes reduced zap's activity. results indicate zap
inhibits hiv-1 recruiting 5' 3' mrna degradation machinery to
specifically promote degradation multiply spliced hiv-1 mrnas.
