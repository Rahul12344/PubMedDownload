the incidence cytomegalovirus (cmv) infection among 107 low birth weight
transfused infants (birth weight less equal 1,500 g) admitted an
intensive care nursery 18 month period evaluated. diagnosis of
cmv infection based specific serologic tests (presence igm, increased
igg elisa technic) identification virus urine. the
first 8 months, infants received untested blood cmv disease occurred 8
infants 44 (18.2%). following 10 months, transfusions
performed 63 infants supposed cmv negative. however, 32 infants
received untested blood due emergency, 5 developed cmv infection
(15.6%). finally, 31 infants received cmv negative blood without case of
cmv infection. data clearly demonstrate that, considering severity of
the cmv disease premature infants, transfusions performed with
cmv negative blood products.
