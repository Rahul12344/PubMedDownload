background/purpose: under-utilization papanicolaou (pap) smear causes gap in
the prevention cervical neoplasms. prospective population-based study was
conducted investigating whether self-sampling human papillomavirus (hpv) test
was feasible under-users pap smear factors associated with
under-screening taiwan.
methods: women pap smear screening > 5 years invited to
participate study. invitation letters educational brochures were
mailed 4% randomly selected eligible women taoyuan city, taiwan, and
responders received hpv self-sampling kit. hpv-positive results
were recalled pap smear colposcopy.
results: march 2010 june 2012, 10,693 women invited, 354
responded (3.3%), 282 (2.6%) gave valid informed consent, answered the
questionnaire, submitted hpv samples. median age enrolled women was
48.1 years. forty-seven women (16.7%) positive hpv test, 14 women
accepted survey find two cin2+. another two cases cin2+ were
identified national registry database. cost direct mailing
self-samplers less done request (from nt$434,866 nt$164,229, 
response rate 5% 15%, respectively, versus nt$683,957 detecting 1
cin2+). reasons attending screening included lack time, embarrassment,
assumed low risk, fear positive results, perceived potential pain. among
the responders, 90.8% found method acceptable.
conclusion: study indicated different approaches (e.g., direct mailing
self-samplers under-users and/or various educational interventions) must be
explored improve coverage populations culture characteristics similar 
to taiwan.
