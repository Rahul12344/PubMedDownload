introduction: infectious agent enters host results febrile
illness. may lead increase morbidity even mortality in
undiagnosed/untreated cases. many aetiological agents lead to
acute febrile illness. among aetiological agents, important bacterial or
viral aetiology.
objective: objective study is: (i) know aetiological agents
responsible acute undifferentiated febrile illness (aufi) serological test
or bacterial culture (ii) know clinical profile aufi.
methodology: total 270 patients enroled study history 
aufi admitted medicine paediatric department january 2015 november
2016 tertiary care hospital central india. blood sample collected for
blood culture, clot culture serological tests immunochromatographic tests
(icts) ict-positive results confirmed respective enzyme-linked
immunosorbent assay (elisa). negative serum samples immunochromatography
were retested disease-specific elisa scrub typhus, dengue and
leptospirosis.
results: 270 patients, 127 (47%) scrub typhus, 33 (12%) were
malaria cases, 47 (17.40%) dengue, 12 (4%) enteric fever, 5 (2%) were
leptospirosis, undiagnosed 18 (6.66%) infections (viz viral,
urinary tract infection, upper lower respiratory tract infection acute
gastroenteritis) accounts 28 (10.37%) cases. also noticed 
was co-infection scrub typhus dengue, leptospirosis scrub typhus.
conclusion: important know cause clinical profile aufis for
their proper management also help prevent morbidity mortality in
aufi cases.
