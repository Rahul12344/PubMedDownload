in response 2009 h1n1 influenza pandemic, public health authorities
launched ambitious vaccination program protect tens millions of
americans virus. april may 2010, institute medicine forum 
on medical public health preparedness catastrophic events hosted series
of 3 regional workshops examine 2009 h1n1 vaccination campaign. the
workshops brought together stakeholders involved distributing dispensing
h1n1 vaccine discuss successes challenges identify strategies to
improve future vaccination programs medical countermeasure dispensing
campaigns. basis presentations discussions followed,
several themes opportunities future efforts identified the
following areas: vaccine supply demand; state local implementation of
centers disease control prevention/advisory committee immunization
practices recommendations, including prioritization vaccination; vaccine
formulations priority groups; opportunities developing partnerships;
opportunities increase seasonal vaccination rates among pregnant women and
health care workers increase acceptance live attenuated nasal spray
vaccine; standardization improvement immunization information management
systems; opportunities simplify, systematize, automate processes and
practices; research needs opportunities.
