hiv-1 epidemic among female bar hotel workers northern tanzania: risk
factors opportunities prevention.

kapiga sh(1), sam ne, shao jf, renjifo b, masenga ej, kiwelu ie, manongi r, fawzi
w, essex m.

author information: 
(1)department population international health, harvard school public
health, boston, massachusetts 02115, usa. skapiga@hsph.harvard.edu

we conducted study determine prevalence risk factors hiv-1
infection among women (n = 312) working bars hotels moshi,
a town northern tanzania. study subjects interviewed obtain
information hiv-1 risk factors examined collect samples the
diagnosis sexually transmitted diseases (stds). prevalence hiv-1 was
26.3% (95% confidence interval [ci], 21.4%-31.2%). multivariate analyses, 
risk hiv-1 increased increasing age (p value, test linear trend
<.001) number sexual partners last 5 years (p value, test
for linear trend <.03). significant predictors male partner
with sexual partners (adjusted odds ratio [aor], 1.92; 95% ci, 1.03-3.60), 
and consuming alcohol >2 days per week (aor, 2.56; 95% ci, 1.12-5.88). risk
of hiv-1 also significantly increased women bacterial vaginosis (aor,
2.37; 95% ci, 1.09-5.13) study subjects herpes simplex virus (hsv)-2 
antibodies (aor, 2.48; 95% ci, 1.24-4.98). results indicate women
working settings increased risk hiv-1. programs aiming at
promoting safer sexual practices control stds urgently needed in
this population. programs address underlying conditions that
facilitate risk behaviors create obstacles women wish to
protect hiv-1.
