background: report case 35-year-old man stroke related to
infective endocarditis (ie) caused kocuria kristinae.
case description: patient chronic hepatitis c virus infection a
former intravenous drug user developed stroke suddenly, three months
duration fever malaise. duplex ultrasonography carotid arteries
(zero level diastolic flow, diastolic reversed flow) focused attention cardiac
valve pathology endocarditis (definite confirmation made by
transesophageal echocardiography). kocuria kristinae grown blood
culture antibiotic therapy administered, according antibiogram, did
not cure infection patient underwent aortic valve replacement with
a mechanical prosthesis debridement mitral valve. one year the
surgery, patient subjective problems neurological findings were
normal.  conclusion: knowledge, first case ie caused by
kocuria kristinae, diagnosed development stroke, where ie 
was suspected based on duplex ultrasonography carotid arteries.  is
the second case infection bacterium could cured by
antibiotics submitted surgical intervention. hippokratia
