objectives: authors studied epidemiological, clinical, outcome
features ebola virus disease patients hospitalized ebola
treatment center (etc) conakry identify clinical factors associated with
death.
materials methods: prospective study conducted march 25 august 
20, 2014. diagnosis ebola virus infection made real-time pcr.
results: ninety patients, positive test result, hospitalized. their
mean age 34.12±14.29 years 63% male patients. worked the
informal sector (38%) medical paramedical staff (physicians 12%,
nurses 6%, laboratory technicians 1%). patients lived conakry
suburbs (74%) boffa (11%). main clinical signs physical asthenia 
(80%) fever (72%). hemorrhagic signs observed 26% patients. the
comparison clinical manifestations showed hiccups (p=0.04), respiratory
distress (p=0.04), hemorrhagic symptoms (p=0.01) frequent among
patients died. malaria (72%) diabetes (2%) frequent
co-morbidities. crude case fatality rate 44% [95% confidence interval
(33-54%)]. average hospital stay 7.96±5.81 days.
conclusion: first ebola outbreak conakry characterized young
age patients, discrete hemorrhagic signs related lethality. control
relies strict use preventive measures.
