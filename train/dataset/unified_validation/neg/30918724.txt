background: postexposure chemoprophylaxis prevent human immunodeficiency
virus (hiv) infection risk health care workers; however routine adoption of
these practices workers limited.
methods: cross-sectional study conducted 311 health care workers of
hiwot fana specialized university hospital february march 2016. data 
was collected using structured self-administered questionnaire analysed
using stata 12.
results: all, 83% participants adequate knowledge postexposure
prophylaxis hiv. respondents heard postexposure prophylaxis
for hiv; however, 37 (22.4%) workers know definition postexposure
prophylaxis. among study participants, majority them, 272 (87.5%), knew
the preferable time initiate postexposure chemoprophylaxis. significant
number workers (43.4%) unfavorable attitude towards postexposure
prophylaxis. among 53 workers potential exposure hiv, 38 (71.7%) took
postexposure chemoprophylaxis 26 (44.8%) completed taking postexposure
prophylaxis correctly.
conclusion: all, health care workers adequate knowledge 
postexposure prophylaxis hiv/aids. result shows significant
number individuals negative attitude poor practice regard to
postexposure prophylaxis. therefore, formal training aims improve
attitudes support improve postexposure prophylaxis implementation and
completion needed. would recommend establishment appropriate
guidelines supply chain ensure availability postexposure
prophylaxis drugs protection healthcare workers potential high
risk exposure hiv.
