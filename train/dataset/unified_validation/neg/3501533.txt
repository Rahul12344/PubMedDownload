the acquired immune deficiency syndrome: report first case malaysia.

goh kl, chua ct, chiew is, soo-hoo ts.

pip: 1st case acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (aids) malaysia was
detected 1987, 1 year ministry health established national aids
task force. patient 45-year-old chinese man malaysian origin had
been living overseas past 30 years. patient denied homosexual
relationships, blood transfusions, intravenous drug use; however, reported 
multiple sexual partners 10 years prior developing aids. hospital
admission, patient acute respiratory distress pneumocystis carinii
was isolated sputum. enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay western
blot tests revealed antibodies human immunodeficiency virus (hiv). t-
helper/t-suppressor cell ratio inverted, 16:51%. antibodies 
cytomegalovirus toxoplasmosis. patient showed rapid clinical improvement
after treatment co-trimoxazole released hospital 2 weeks
after admission. although 1st case full-blown aids malaysia,
individuals antibodies hiv identified.
