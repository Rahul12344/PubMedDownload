this paper describes biochemical study making complexes the
nucleoprotein influenza viruses b (a/np b/np) small rnas (polyuc
rnas 5 24 nucleotides (nt)), starting monomeric proteins. used
negative stain electron microscopy, size exclusion chromatography-multi-angle
laser light scattering (sec-malls) analysis, fluorescence anisotropy
measurements show np-rna complexes evolve. proteins make small
oligomers 24-nt rnas, trimers a/np, dimers, tetramers, larger
complexes b/np. shorter rnas, affinities np same
range 50 mm nacl, showing rnas bind site. affinity of
b/np 24-nt rna change salt. however, affinity a/np for
a 24-nt rna lower 150 300 mm nacl, suggesting rna binds to
another site, either protomer neighbour protomer. our
fluorescence anisotropy experiments, used 6-fluorescein amidite (fam)-labelled
rnas. using (uc)₆-fam(3') rna 150 mm nacl, observed interesting
phenomenon gives macromolecular complexes similar ribonucleoprotein
particles purified viruses.
