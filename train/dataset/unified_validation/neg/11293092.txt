introduction: segmental motor paralysis limbs (smp) complicates 2-3% of
the cases cutaneous herpes zoster. viral invasion inflammation the
motor neurons anterior horn cells varicella-zoster virus (vvz)
causes clinical weakness time site cutaneous eruption.
objectives: analyze clinical findings, complementary investigations and
functional prognosis patients smp brachial plexus lumbosacral
levels.
patients methods: made retrospective study 10 patients smp
admitted hospital universitario gregorio maranon de madrid during
1989-1999, aged 38 84 years (6 women, 4 men). neurological
examination done, including muscle balance, complementary studies including
microbiology (serum csf serology, viral pcr-adn), neurophysiology using mnr
of spine plexuses functional prognosis nds, nss rankin
scales.
results: close relationship dermatome myotome involvement 
(90%). brachial lumbosacral plexuses equally affected (50%). plasma 
and csf vvz serology positive 50% cases, permitting diagnosis 
patient cutaneous lesions (zoster sine herpete). denervation the
myotomes involved paraspinal muscles shown neurophysiological
studies. cases functional improvement, complete functional
recovery 80% cases 12 months.
conclusions: vvz considered amongst aetiologies smp, even the
absence cutaneous lesions (zoster sine herpete). smp coincides time and
place dermatome lesions. patients complete functional
recovery within 12 months.
