background: number people newly infected human immunodeficiency virus
(hiv) decreasing sub-saharan africa, prevalence infection 
remains unacceptably high among young people. despite alarming pervasiveness 
of virus, young people region continue engage risky sexual
behaviors including unprotected sexual intercourse. developed countries,
parents play important roles protecting young people behaviors, 
but evidence regarding impact parental involvement still limited in
sub-saharan africa. therefore, conducted study examine magnitude
of risky sexual behaviors association parental monitoring parental
communication condom use last sexual intercourse among secondary school
students dar es salaam, tanzania.
methods: conducted cross-sectional study among 2,217 male female
students aged 15 24 years 12 secondary schools dar es salaam. from
october november 2011, collected data using self-administered
questionnaire. multiple logistic regression analyses conducted examine
the association parental monitoring parental communication condom use
at last sexual intercourse, adjusting potential confounders.
results: total 665 (30.3%) secondary school students reported sexually
active within year prior data collection. among them, 41.7% multiple
sexual partners, 10.5% concurrent sexual partners, 41.1% use a
condom last sexual intercourse. higher level parental monitoring was
associated increased likelihood condom use last sexual intercourse
among male students (aor: 1.56, 95% ci: 1.05-2.32; p = 0.03) among female
students (aor: 1.54, 95% ci: 0.71-3.37; p = 0.28). association between
parental communication condom use last sexual intercourse among male 
and female students statistically significant.
conclusions: high level parental monitoring associated more
consistent condom use among male students dar es salaam, tanzania -- many of
whom engaged high-risk sexual behaviors multiple sexual
partnerships, concurrent sexual partnerships, unprotected sexual intercourse 
in past one year. interventions thus strengthened reduce
multiple sexual partnerships, concurrent sexual partnerships, improve
parental monitoring among students toward increasing condom use.
