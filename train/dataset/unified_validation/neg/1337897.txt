an epidemic acute haemorrhagic conjunctivitis caused coxsackie a24 variant.

broor s(1), kishore j, dogra v, satapathy g, seth p.

author information: 
(1)department microbiology, india institute medical sciences, new
delhi.

an epidemic acute haemorrhagic conjunctivitis (ahc) caused variant of
coxsackie a24 (cox a24) occurred delhi august september 1988. cox
a24 antigen detected indirect immunofluorescence (ifa) conjunctival
cell smears 13 38 (34.2%) patients studied. virus isolated from
conjunctival swabs 11 (28.9%) patients isolates neutralized by
cox a24 antiserum. five virus strains sent virology division centres for
disease control, atlanta, usa, confirmed cox a24 variant. enterovirus
type 70 (ev70) demonstrable either ifa neutralization tests.
conjunctival swabs 10 healthy laboratory controls show evidence 
of ev70 cox a24 virus antigens.
