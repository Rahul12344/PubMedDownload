comparison high-resolution computed tomography findings pseudomonas
aeruginosa pneumonia cytomegalovirus pneumonia.

objective: compare pulmonary high-resolution ct (hrct) findings patients
with pseudomonas aeruginosa pneumonia hrct findings patients with
cytomegalovirus (cmv) pneumonia.
methods: studied 124 patients (77 men, 47 women; age range, 20-89 years; mean 
age, 65.4 years) p. aeruginosa pneumonia 44 patients (22 men, 22 women; 
age range, 36-86 years; mean age, 63.2 years) cmv pneumonia.
results: ct findings consolidation (p < 0.005), bronchial wall thickening
(p < 0.001), cavity (p < 0.05), pleural effusion (p < 0.001) were
significantly frequent patients p. aeruginosa pneumonia in
those cmv pneumonia. centrilobular nodules, crazy-paving appearance, and
nodules significantly frequent patients cmv pneumonia in
those p. aeruginosa pneumonia (all p < 0.001).
conclusion: pulmonary hrct findings, bronchial wall thickening,
crazy-paving appearance, nodules may useful distinguishing p.
aeruginosa pneumonia cmv pneumonia.
key points: distinguishing pseudomonas aeruginosa pneumonia cytomegalovirus 
pneumonia important. characteristic features underlying conditions are
present pneumonia species. bronchial wall thickening cavities are
more frequent pseudomonas aeruginosa pneumonia. nodules crazy-paving
appearance frequent cytomegalovirus pneumonia.
