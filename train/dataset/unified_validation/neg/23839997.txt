background: hajj oldest largest annual mass gathering world
and may increase risk spread respiratory viruses.
methods: performed prospective survey among cohort pilgrims departing
from marseille, france, mecca kingdom saudi arabia (ksa) the
2012 hajj season. nasal swabs collected participants tested 11 
respiratory viruses real-time reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction.
results: 165 participants sampled departing ksa, 8 (4.8%) were
positive least 1 virus (5 rhinovirus, 1 influenza c, 1 adenovirus, 1
enterovirus). seventy symptomatic pilgrims underwent additional nasal swabs
during pilgrimage ksa, 27 (38.6%) positive at
least 1 virus (19 rhinovirus, 6 influenza a, 1 influenza c, 1 respiratory
syncytial virus b, 1 metapneumovirus, 1 adenovirus, 1 enterovirus). 
significantly higher 4.8% positive departing ksa
(p < .001). 154 pilgrims sampled leaving ksa, 17 (11%) were
positive least 1 virus (13 rhinovirus, 3 adenovirus, 2 influenza b, 1 
enterovirus), also significantly higher percentage positive
pilgrims (4.8%), departing ksa (p = .040).
conclusions: study suggests rapid acquisition respiratory viruses among
pilgrims stay ksa, notably rhinovirus, highlights
the potential spreading infections pilgrims' home countries upon 
their return.
