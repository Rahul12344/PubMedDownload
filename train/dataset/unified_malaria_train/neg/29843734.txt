background: recent spread artemisinin (art)-resistant plasmodium
falciparum represents emerging global threat public health. southeast
asia, c580y mutation kelch13 (k13) dominant mutation of
art-resistant p. falciparum. therefore, simple method detection of
c580y mutation urgently needed enable widespread routine surveillance in
the field. aim study develop new diagnostic procedure the
c580y mutation using loop-mediated isothermal amplification (lamp) combined 
the minion nanopore sequencer.
results: lamp assay k13 gene p. falciparum detect c580y
mutation successfully developed. detection limit procedure 10
copies reference plasmid harboring k13 gene within 60 min. thereafter,
amplicon sequencing lamp products using minion nanopore sequencer 
performed clarify nucleotide sequences gene. c580y mutation was
identified based sequence data collected minion reads 30 min after
the start sequencing. further, clinical evaluation lamp assay 34
human blood samples collected patients p. falciparum malaria in
indonesia revealed positive detection rate 100%. lamp amplicons to
12 specimens simultaneously sequenced using minion. results of
sequencing consistent conventional pcr sanger
sequencing protocol. procedures dna extraction variant calling were
completed within 3 h. c580y mutation found among 34 p.
falciparum isolates indonesia.
conclusions: innovative method combining lamp minion enable simple,
rapid, high-sensitivity detection c580y mutation p. falciparum,
even resource-limited situations developing countries.
