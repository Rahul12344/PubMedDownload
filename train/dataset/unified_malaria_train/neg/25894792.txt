aspidospermine indole alkaloid biological properties associated with
combating parasites included genera plasmodium, leishmania and
trypanossoma. present study evaluated cytotoxicity (resazurin test),
genotoxicity (comet assay) mechanism action (gene expression analysis via 
qrt-pcr) alkaloid human hepg2 cells. results demonstrated that
treatment aspidospermine cytotoxic (starting 75 μm) and
genotoxic (starting 50 μm). significant modulation the
expression following genes: gstp1 gpx1 (xenobiotic metabolism); cat
(oxidative stress); tp53 ccna2 (cell cycle); hspa5, ern1, eif2ak3 traf2
(endoplasmic reticulum stress); casp8, casp9, casp3, casp7, bcl-2, bcl-xl bax and
bax (apoptosis); pcbp4, ercc4, ogg1, rad21 mlh1 (dna repair). a
concentration 50 μm (non-cytotoxic, genotoxic), significant
increase expression cyp1a1 (xenobiotic metabolism) apc (cell
cycle), concentration 100 μm, significant increase the
expression cyp1a1 (xenobiotic metabolism), gadd153 (endoplasmic reticulum
stress) sod (oxidative stress) detected, repression the
expression gr (xenobiotic metabolism oxidative stress). results of
treatment aspidospermine 100 μm concentration (the dose indicated in
the literature achieve 89 % reduction growth l. amazonensis) suggest
that increased oxidative stress unfolded protein response (upr) occurred
in hepg2 cells. therapeutic use aspidospermine (antiparasitic),
chemical alteration molecule achieve lower cytotoxicity/genotoxicity 
in host cells recommended.
