background: december 2010, plasmodium vivax malaria outbreak occurred among 
french forces involved mission control illegal gold mining french
guiana. findings epidemiological entomological investigations
conducted outbreak presented here.
methods: data related malaria cases reported french armed forces
epidemiological surveillance system collected epidemic period
from december 2010 april 2011. retrospective cohort study conducted to
identify presumed contamination sites. anopheles mosquitoes sampled the
identified sites using mosquito magnet cdc light traps. specimens were
identified morphologically confirmed using molecular methods (sequencing of
its2 gene and/or barcoding). anopheles infections plasmodium falciparum 
p. vivax tested enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay real-time pcr.
results: seventy-two p. vivax malaria cases reported (three mixed p.
falciparum/p. vivax infections), leading global attack rate 26.5%
(72/272). lack compliance vector control measures doxycycline
chemoprophylaxis reported patients. two illegal gold mining sites located 
in remote areas primary forest identified places contamination.
in all, 595 anopheles females caught 528 specimens formally
identified: 305 anopheles darlingi, 145 anopheles nuneztovari s.l., 63 anopheles 
marajoara 15 anopheles triannulatus s.l. three an. darlingi infected 
p. falciparum (infection rate: 1.1%) four an. marajoara p. vivax
(infection rate: 6.4%).
discussion: main drivers outbreak lack adherence by
military personnel malaria prevention measures high level malaria
transmission illegal gold mining sites. anopheles marajoara clearly
implicated malaria transmission first time french guiana. high 
infection rates observed confirm illegal gold mining sites must be
considered high level malaria transmission areas territory.
conclusions: illegal gold mining activities challenging control of
malaria french guiana. collaboration neighbouring countries necessary 
to take account mobile populations gold miners. malaria control
strategies french armed forces must adapted p. vivax malaria and
sylvatic anopheles species.
