blastocystis common protozoan colonizing gut vertebrates. it
modulates human digestive microbiota absence inflammation and
gastrointestinal disease. although associated human diseases,
including inflammatory bowel disease, pathogenicity remains controversial.
this study aimed assess influence blastocystis gut bacterial
communities healthy children. conducted cross-sectional study 147
blastocystis-colonized 149 blastocystis-noncolonized malian children, with
blastocystis colonization assessed real-time pcr gut microbial communities
characterized via 16s rrna gene (illumina miseq) sequencing bioinformatics
analysis. gut microbiota diversity higher blastocystis-colonized
compared blastocystis-noncolonized children. phyla firmicutes,
elusimicrobia, lentisphaerae, euryarchaeota higher in
blastocystis-colonized children, whereas actinobacteria, proteobacteria,
unassigned bacteria, deinococcus-thermus higher in
blastocystis-noncolonized children. moreover, faecalibacterium prausnitzii
(family ruminococcaceae) roseburia sp. (family lachnospiraceae) abundance was
higher blastocystis-colonized children. conclude blastocystis
colonization significantly associated higher diversity gut
bacterial communities healthy children, associated the
presence potentially pathogenic bacteria human gut.
