plasmodium falciparum neutral metallo-aminopeptidase (pfam1), member m1 
family metallo proteases, promising target malaria, devastating
human parasitic disease. report high-level expression pfam1 in
escherichia coli bl21. optimized gene, codon adaptation index an
average g/c content higher native gene, synthesized cloned in
the ptrchis2b vector. optimal expression achieved induction 1mm iptg 
at 37°c 18h. allowed obtaining 100mg recombinant pfam1 (rpfam1) per l
of culture medium; 19% e. coli soluble protein mass rpfam1.
rpfam1, fused amino-terminal 6×his tag, purified single step by
immobilized metal ion affinity chromatography. protein showed limited
signs proteolytic degradation, step increased purity 27-fold. the
kinetic characteristics rpfam1, neutral optimal ph, preference for
substrates basic hydrophobic amino acids p1 position, an
inhibition profile typical metallo-aminopeptidases, inhibition zn(2+)
excess, similar native pfam1. thus optimized an
expression system useful identifying new pfam1 inhibitors.
