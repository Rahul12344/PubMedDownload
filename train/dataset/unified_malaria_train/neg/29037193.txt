erratum in
    malar j. 2017 nov 14;16(1):464.

background: malaria programmes use plasmodium falciparum histidine-rich protein-2
(pfhrp2) based rapid diagnostic tests (rdts) malaria diagnosis. deletion 
of target antigen could potentially lead misdiagnosis, delayed treatment 
and continuation active transmission.
methods: plasmodium falciparum isolates (n = 1162) collected southern
mozambique assessed rdts, microscopy and/or 18srrna qpcr. pfhrp2 and
pfhrp3 deletions investigated isolates individuals negative
by rdt positive microscopy and/or qpcr (n = 69) using gene-specific pcrs, 
with kelch13 pcr parasite dna control.
results: lack pfhrp2 pcr amplification observed one 69 isolates 
subjected molecular analysis [1.45% (95% ci 0.3-7.8%)].
conclusions: low prevalence pfhrp2 deletions suggests rdts will
detect vast majority p. falciparum infections. nevertheless, active
surveillance changing deletion frequencies required.
