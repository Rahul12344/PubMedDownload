background: kingdom saudi arabia seeking malaria eradication. malaria
transmission low last years. discovered cases of
plasmodium falciparum infection assigned treatment protocol of
artemisinin-based combination therapy, consists artesunate addition
to sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine rather traditional chloroquine, has
high resistance rates worldwide. study aims investigate presence of
different gene mutations concerning anti-malarial drug resistance (pfdhfr,
pfdhps, pfmdr1, pfcrt, pfcytb, pfkelch13) identify whether drug-resistant
alleles present area kingdom whether country's
treatment protocol still suitable plasmodium bearing resistance mutation
[corrected].
methods: blood samples collected patients suffering symptoms
suggesting malaria coming king faisal hospital, taif, february august 
2016. diagnosis performed giemsa-stained thin thick blood films, rapid
diagnostic test pcr. positive p. falciparum samples subjected to
series pcr amplification reactions targeting genes related drug
resistance (pfdhfr, pfdhps, pfmdr1, pfcrt, pfcytb, pfketch13).
results: twenty-six cases positives, 13 infected p. falciparum, of
those, 4 cases autochthonous, 13 plasmodium vivax. results of
the gene mutation detection confirmed mutation related to
resistance artemisinin atovaquone, hand chloroquine resistance
alleles detected 31% samples. moreover, point mutations pfdhfr 
and pfdhps genes, related resistance antifolate drugs, detected all
characterized samples.
conclusions: haplotypes p. falciparum western region kingdom 
saudi arabia exhibit high resistance antifolate drugs. results
should extensively discussed planning modify anti-malarial drug
protocols future.
