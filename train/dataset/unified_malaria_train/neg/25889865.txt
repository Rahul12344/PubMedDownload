background: malaria situation worsening angola, partly due to
armed conflict recent past drug-resistant plasmodium falciparum.
malaria transmission heterogeneous within country, data on
drug-resistant malaria different parts country incomplete. aim 
of present study evaluate resistance 4-aminoquinolines and
antifolate drugs p. falciparum isolates collected benguela province,
central angola, using molecular markers.
methods: fingerprick capillary blood collected asymptomatic children
aged less 15 years old household survey around balombo town 
in 2010-2011. samples screened p. falciparum nested pcr. molecular
markers (p. falciparum dihydrofolate reductase [pfdhfr], p. falciparum
dihydropteroate synthase [pfdhps], p. falciparum chloroquine resistance
transporter [pfcrt], p. falciparum multidrug-resistance gene 1 [pfmdr1]) were
sequenced determine key codons associated drug resistance.
results: total 60 blood samples positive p. falciparum. most
isolates successful pcr amplification mutant pfdhfr alleles, either
double mutant aicni (69%) triple mutant airni (21%) haplotypes. a16v, s108t,
and i164l substitutions found. many isolates carriers of
either sgkaa (60%) agkaa (27%) pfdhps haplotype. k540e substitution was
absent. two pfcrt haplotypes: wild-type cvmnk (11%) mutant
cviet (89%). wild-type pfmdr1 nysnd haplotype found 19% isolates,
whereas single mutant pfmdr1 yysnd nfsnd haplotypes occurred 48% 11%, 
respectively. double mutant pfmdr1 haplotypes (yfsnd yysny) occurred rarely.
conclusions: results suggest high prevalence mutant pfcrt cviet
haplotype agreement low clinical efficacy chloroquine observed in
earlier studies double pfdhfr mutant aicni single pfdhps mutant 
sgkaa currently predominant haplotypes associated antifolate
resistance benguela province. hallmark clinical resistance observed 
east africa, i.e. triple pfdhfr mutant haplotype (airni) double pfdhps mutant
haplotype (sgeaa), absent. molecular findings need further
evaluated parallel clinical studies, particular efficacy of
intermittent preventive treatment using sulphadoxine-pyrimethamine pregnant
women artesunate-amodiaquine uncomplicated malaria.
