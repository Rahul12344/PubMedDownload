the arribalzagia series anopheles subgenus comprises morphologically
similar species members species complexes makes correct species
identification difficult. therefore, aim work discriminate the
morphospecies arribalzagia series present colombia using multilocus
approach based its2, coi cad sequences. specimens arribalzagia
series collected 32 localities nine departments allocated seven
species. individual concatenated bayesian analyses showed high support for
each species reinforced previous report apicimacula species
complex distribution pacific coast northwestern colombia. in
addition, new molecular operational taxonomic unit-motu identified, herein 
denominated near anopheles peryassui, providing support existence a
peryassui species complex. further, cad gene, recently used for
anopheles taxonomy phylogeny, demonstrated power resolving
phylogenetic relationships among species arribalzagia series. the
divergence times species correspond early pliocene the
miocene. considering epidemiological importance species series
and co-occurrence malaria endemic regions colombia, their
discrimination constitutes important step vector incrimination control
in country.
