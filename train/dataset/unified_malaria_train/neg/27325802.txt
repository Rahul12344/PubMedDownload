estimates malaria transmission intensity (mti) typically rely upon microscopy 
or rapid diagnostic testing (rdt). however, methods less sensitive than
nucleic acid amplification techniques may underestimate parasite prevalence. 
we compared microscopy, rdt, polymerase chain reaction (pcr) the
diagnosis plasmodium falciparum parasitemia part mti study 800
children adults conducted lilongwe, malawi. pcr detected cases of
parasitemia microscopy rdt. age less 5 years predicted parasitemia
detected pcr alone (adjusted odds ratio = 1.61, 95% confidence interval =
1.09-2.38, wald p = 0.02). addition, identified one p. falciparum parasite 
with false-negative rdt result due suspected deletion the
histidine-rich protein 2 (hrp2) gene used novel, ultrasensitive pcr assay
to detect low-level parasitemia missed traditional pcr. molecular methods
should considered use future transmission studies supplement to
rdt microscopy.
