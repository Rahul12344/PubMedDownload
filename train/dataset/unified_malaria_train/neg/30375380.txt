malaria reduces host fitness survival pathogen-mediated damage and
inflammation. disease tolerance mechanisms counter negative effects without
decreasing pathogen load. here, demonstrate four different mouse
models malaria, adrenal hormones confer disease tolerance protect 
early death, independently parasitemia. surprisingly, adrenalectomy
differentially affects malaria-induced inflammation increasing circulating
cytokines inflammation brain liver lung.
furthermore, without affecting transcription hepatic gluconeogenic
enzymes, adrenalectomy causes exhaustion hepatic glycogen and
insulin-independent lethal hypoglycemia upon infection. hypoglycemia 
prevented glucose administration tnf-Î± neutralization. contrast,
treatment synthetic glucocorticoid (dexamethasone) prevents the
hypoglycemia, lowers cerebral cytokine expression increases survival rates.
overall, conclude malaria, adrenal hormones protect against
lung liver inflammation. instead, prevent excessive systemic brain
inflammation severe hypoglycemia, thereby contributing tolerance.
