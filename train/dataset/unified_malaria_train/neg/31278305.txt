plasmodium infections endemic areas often asymptomatic, caused by
different species contribute significantly transmission. performed a
cross-sectional study february/march 2016 including 840 individuals ≥ 1 year
living rural gabon (ngounié moyen-ogooué). plasmodium parasitemia was
measured high-sensitive, real-time quantitative pcr. randomly chosen
subset p. falciparum infections, gametocyte carriage prevalence of
chloroquine-resistant genotypes analysed. 618/834 (74%) individuals were
positive plasmodium 18s-rrna gene amplification, 553 (66.3%) carried
p. falciparum, 193 (23%) p. malariae, 74 (8.9%) p. ovale curtisi 38 (4.6%)
p.ovale wallikeri. non-falciparum infections mostly presented mixed
infections. p. malariae monoinfected individuals significantly older (median
age: 60 years) coinfected (20 years) p. falciparum monoinfected
individuals (23 years). p. falciparum gametocyte carriage confirmed in
109/223 (48.9%) individuals, prevalence chloroquine-resistant genotypes was
high (298/336, 89%), including four infections new svmnk genotype. in
rural gabon, plasmodium infections endemic species frequent,
emphasizing malaria control efforts shall cover asymptomatic infections also
including non-falciparum infections aiming eradication.
