background: emergence transnational spread artemisinin resistance in
plasmodium falciparum greater mekong sub-region (gms) serious threat 
to malaria elimination region could present threat malaria
control africa. recently, lao government adopted goal malaria
elimination 2030, monitoring artemisinin-resistant malaria within
the country indispensable. study's objectives assess the
distribution k13 mutations laos.
methods: plasmodium falciparum isolates (n = 1151) collected five
southern provinces laos 2015 2016, three isolates the
northernmost province bordering china 2017. polymorphisms k13 gene 
two flanking regions analysed estimate relationship among isolates.
results: five southern provinces, overall 55.5% isolates possessed 
artemisinin-resistant mutations k13 gene (c580y, p574l, r539t, y493h). the
c580y predominant mutation (87.2%). frequencies k13 mutations 
were heterogeneous five southern provinces, clear tendency
showing highest frequency south (72.5%) lower degree when
moving northward (28.0%). three isolates lao-chinese border also
possessed c580y mutation. analysis flanking loci demonstrated that
these three isolates genetically close resistant strains originating
from western cambodia.
conclusions: artemisinin resistance observed rapidly increasing and
spreading northwards laos reached chinese border. lao
and chinese governments, well international community, make
dedicated efforts contain spread k13 mutations within laos the
gms.
