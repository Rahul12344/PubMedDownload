background: sabang municipality, aceh province, indonesia, plans initiate a
malaria elimination programme 2013. baseline survey distribution of
malaria municipality conducted lay foundations an
evidence-based programme assess island's readiness begin the
elimination process.
methods: entire population municipality screened malaria
infection g6pd deficiency. specimens collected included blood slides, blots
and tubes selected households.
results discussion: samples collected 16,229 residents. microscopic
examination blood smears revealed 12 malaria infections; 10 with
plasmodium falciparum 2 plasmodium vivax. confirm parasite
prevalence, polymerase chain reaction (pcr) diagnosis performed entire
positive cases microscopy randomized 10% microscopically negative
blood samples. pcr revealed additional 11 subjects malaria; one p.
falciparum infection village paya keunekai, nine p. vivax
infections one mixed p. falciparum/p. vivax infection village of
batee shok. overall slide positivity rate 0.074% (ci 95%: 0.070-0.078)
and pcr corrected prevalence 0,590% (ci 95%: 0.582-0.597). analysis 937 blood 
samples g6pd deficiency revealed two subjects (0.2%) deficient g6pd.
analysis several genes parasite, pfdhfr, pfdhps, pfmdr1, pfcrt,
pfmsp1, pfmsp2, pvdhfr, pvdhps, pvmdr1 host gene, g6pd gene revealed 
that p. falciparum p. vivax carried mutation associated with
chloroquine resistance.
conclusion: malariometric host genetic analysis indicated low
prevalence malaria g6pd deficiency population sabang
municipality. nevertheless, malaria cases clustered three rural villages 
and efforts malaria elimination sabang particularly focused on
those three villages.
