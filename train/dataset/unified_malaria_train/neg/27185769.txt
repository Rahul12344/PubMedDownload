a nanoparticle-based assay detection quantification plasmodium
falciparum histidine-rich protein 2 (hrp2) urine serum reported. the
assay uses magnetic beads conjugated anti-hrp2 antibody protein capture 
and concentration, antibody-conjugated quantum dots optical detection.
western blot analysis demonstrated magnetic beads allow concentration of
hrp2 protein urine 20-fold. concentration effect achieved because
large volume urine incubated beads, magnetic separation be
easily performed minutes isolate beads containing hrp2 protein. magnetic
beads quantum dots conjugated anti-hrp2 antibodies allows detection of
low concentrations hrp2 protein (0.5 ng/ml), quantification range
of 33-2,000 ng/ml corresponding range associated non-severe severe
malaria. assay easily adapted noninvasive point-of-care test for
classification severe malaria.
