background: recent studies described natural human infections the
non-human primate parasites plasmodium knowlesi plasmodium cynomolgi. in
southeast asia, mosquitoes anopheles leucosphyrus group bite humans
and monkeys forest thus offer possible route plasmodium species 
to bridge species barrier. study analysed species composition 
of malarial sporozoites infecting salivary glands anopheles dirus order
to determine potential role bridge vectors plasmodium parasites 
monkeys humans.
methods: mosquitoes collected forest forest fringe area khanh 
phu commune human-baited landing collection. anopheles species determined
on basis morphologic features. sporozoite-infected salivary glands were
applied filter paper dried ambient atmosphere, storage in
closed vials 4-6 °c. detection identification plasmodium species in
salivary glands carried nested-pcr small subunit ribosomal rna
gene.
results: six species plasmodium parasites detected pcr, p.
vivax common, followed p. knowlesi, p. inui, p. cynomolgi, p.
coatneyi p. falciparum. twenty-six 79 sporozoite infected mosquitoes
showed multiple infections, combination p. vivax one
or non-human primate plasmodium species.
conclusions: results suggest humans overnighting forest are
frequently inoculated human non-human primate malaria parasites,
leading situation conducive emergence novel zoonotic malaria.
