introduction: despite efforts control malaria, around 10% world
population risk acquiring disease. plasmodium falciparum accounts
for majority severe cases deaths. malaria control programs failed
due therapeutic failure first-line antimalarials parasite
resistance. thus, new better therapeutic alternatives required. proteomic
analysis allows determination protein expression levels drug pressure,
leading identification new therapeutic drug targets their
mechanisms action.
objective: aim study analyze qualitatively expression of
p.falciparum trophozoite proteins (strain itg2), exposure antimalarial
drugs, proteomic approach.
materials methods: vitro cultured synchronized parasites treated with
quinine, mefloquine natural antiplasmodial diosgenone. protein extracts
were prepared analyzed two-dimensional electrophoresis. differentially
expressed proteins selected identified maldi-tof mass spectrometry.
results: following proteins identified among differentially
expressed parasite presence drugs tested: enolase
(pf10_0155), calcium-binding protein (pf11_0098), chaperonin (pfl0740c), host
cell invasion protein (pf10_0268) proteins related redox processes
(mal8p1.17). findings consistent results previous studies where
the parasite submitted pressure antimalarial drugs.
conclusion: observed changes p. falciparum trophozoite protein profile
induced antimalarial drugs involved proteins mainly related general
stress response.
