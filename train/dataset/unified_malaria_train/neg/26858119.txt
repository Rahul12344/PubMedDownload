background: pyronaridine-artesunate combination one recent
oral artemisinin-based therapeutic combinations (acts) recommended the
treatment uncomplicated p. falciparum malaria. emergence p. falciparum 
resistance artemisinin recently developed southeast asia. little data
are available association pyronaridine susceptibility and
polymorphisms genes involved antimalarial drug resistance. objective of
the present study investigate association ex vivo responses to
pyronaridine k76t mutation pfcrt gene p. falciparum isolates.
methods: assessment ex vivo susceptibility pyronaridine performed
on 296 p. falciparum isolates using standard 42-h 3h-hypoxanthine uptake
inhibition method. k76t mutation also investigated.
results: pyronaridine ic50 (inhibitory concentration 50 %) ranged 0.55
to 80.0 nm. ex vivo responses pyronaridine significantly associated 
the k76t mutation (p-value = 0.020). reduced susceptibility pyronaridine, 
defined ic50 > 60 nm, significantly associated k76t mutation
(p-value = 0.004). using bayesian mixture modelling approach, pyronaridine 
ic50 classified three components: component (ic50 median 15.9 nm),
component b (ic50 median 34.2 nm) component c (ic50 median 63.3 nm). k76t
mutation represented 46.3% isolates component a, 47.2% the
isolates component b 73.3% isolates component c
(p-value = 0.021).
conclusion: results showed ex vivo reduced susceptibility to
pyronaridine, i.e., ic50 > 60 nm, associated k76t mutation.
