background: anopheles (kerteszia) cruzii (diptera: culicidae) primary vector
of human simian malaria parasites southern southeastern brazil.
earlier studies using chromosome inversions, isoenzymes number molecular
markers suggested an. cruzii species complex.
results: study, multilocus approach using six loci, three circadian
clock genes three encoding ribosomal proteins, carried investigate
in detail genetic differentiation an. cruzii populations
from florianópolis-santa catarina (southern brazil) itatiaia-rio de janeiro
states (southeastern brazil). analyses performed first comparing
florianópolis itatiaia, comparing two putative sympatric
incipient species itatiaia (itatiaia itatiaia b). analysis
revealed high fst values florianópolis itatiaia (considering itatiaia
a b together) also sympatric itatiaia itatiaia b,
irrespective function. also, using im program, strong indication 
of migration found florianópolis itatiaia (considering itatiaia a
and b together) using loci together, itatiaia itatiaia b,
the results show evidence migration direction itatiaia b.
conclusions: results multilocus analysis indicate florianópolis
and itatiaia represent different species an. cruzii complex diverged 
around 0.6 mya, also itatiaia sample composed two sympatric
incipient species b, diverged around 0.2 mya. asymmetric
introgression found latter two species despite strong divergence 
in loci.
