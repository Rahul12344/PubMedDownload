aim: benin, artemisinin-based combination therapy (act) recommended
as first-line treatment uncomplicated plasmodium falciparum malaria since
2004. emergence southeast asia parasites resistant to
artemisinins poses serious threat global control disease. the
presence artemisinin resistance genotypes parasite populations benin 
currently unknown. present study investigated prevalence relevant
k13-propeller gene polymorphisms parasite isolates north-western
region benin.
method: plasmodium falciparum isolates collected children a
confirmed diagnosis malaria aged 6 months 5 years two towns, cobly and
djougou, north-western part benin. study conducted the
rainy season july november 2014 local health facilities. the
k13-propeller gene amplified parasite isolates using nested pcr and
subsequently sequenced.
results: total 108 children recruited study. efficiency of
amplification reactions 72% (78/108). propeller domain k13 gene
was successfully sequenced 78 p. falciparum isolates; wild
type polymorphisms detectable.
conclusion: absence mutations k13 gene indicates p. falciparum
parasite populations study area still fully susceptible to
artemisinins.
