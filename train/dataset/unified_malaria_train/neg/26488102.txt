[13-year old girl fever 6 months returning sub-saharan africa].

[article german]

erhardt j(1), frank m(1).

author information: 
(1)institut für tropenmedizin und humanparasitologie, universitätsklinikum
tübingen.

history admission findings: 13-year-old girl presented regular fevers,
6 months prolonged trip africa. patient reported relapsing
fevers 48 hour intervals. febrile episode followed pronounced
fatigue subsequent recovery back usual state health. reported
having taken weekly mefloquine trip africa.
investigations: labortory evaluation revealed hemoltytic anemia (hemoglobin:
10.8 g / dl, normal range: 12.3-16.0; haptoglobin: < 13 mg / dl, normal range
38-205). abdominal ultrasound showed marked splenomegaly (diameter:
> 13.1 cm).
diagnosis, treatment course: peripheral blood film showed plasmodium
parasites marked stippling. pcr sequenicing ribosomal rna gene
identified plasmodium ovale. patient responded well oral chloroquine
therapy laboratory parameters normalized within 8 days. determination
of normal glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase activity 2-week-long therapy with
primaquine initiated (0,3 mg / kg per kg bodyweight primaquine base daily 
for 14 days) eliminate hyponozoite stage parasite.
conclusions: currently used prophylacic agents malaria (mefloquine,
atovaquone / proguanil hydrochloride, doxyxycline) prevent chronic liver
stage infection (hypnozoite stage) plasmodium ovale plasmodium vivax.
after chemoprophylaxis tertian malaria due plasmodium vivax plasmodium ovale
can occur. therefore, tertian malaria always considered febrile
individuals returned trip tropics even chemoprophylaxis 
taken.
