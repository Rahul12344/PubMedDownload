background: anopheles nuneztovari sensu lato comprises cryptic species in
northern south america, brazilian populations encompass distinct genetic 
lineages within brazilian amazon region. study investigated, based on
two molecular markers, whether lineages might actually deserve species
status.
methods: specimens collected five localities brazilian amazon,
including manaus, careiro castanho autazes, state amazonas;
tucuruí, state pará; abacate da pedreira, state amapá,
and analysed coi gene (barcode region) 12 microsatellite loci.
phylogenetic analyses performed using maximum likelihood (ml) approach. 
intra inter samples genetic diversity estimated using population
genetics analyses, genetic groups identified means ml,
bayesian factorial correspondence analyses bayesian analysis of
population structure.
results: barcode region dataset (n = 103) generated 27 haplotypes. the
haplotype network suggested three lineages. ml tree retrieved five
monophyletic groups. group clustered specimens manaus careiro
castanho, majority autazes abacate da pedreira. group ii
clustered specimens abacate da pedreira autazes
and tucuruí. group iii clustered specimens tucuruí (lineage iii),
strongly supported (97 %). groups iv v clustered specimens a. nuneztovari 
s.s. a. dunhami, strongly (98 %) weakly (70 %) supported, respectively.
in second phylogenetic analysis, sequences genbank, identified a.
goeldii, clustered groups ii, group iii. genetic distances
(kimura-2 parameters) among groups ranged 1.60 % (between ii) to
2.32 % (between iii). microsatellite data revealed high
intra-population genetic variability. genetic distances showed highest and
significant values (p = 0.005) tucuruí samples, and
between abacate da pedreira samples. genetic distances,
bayesian (structure baps) analyses fca suggested three distinct
biological groups, supporting barcode region results.
conclusions: two markers revealed three genetic lineages a. nuneztovari
s.l. brazilian amazon region. lineages ii may represent genetically 
distinct groups species within a. goeldii. lineage iii may represent new
species, distinct a. goeldii group, may ancestral the
brazilian amazon. may differences plasmodium susceptibility and
should therefore investigated further.
