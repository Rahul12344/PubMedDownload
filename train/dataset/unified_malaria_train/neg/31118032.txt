background: malaria, one world's greatest public health challenges, 
endemic disease stable transmission togo. combating malaria requires an
effective vector control. study provides temporal data insecticide
resistance status major malaria vector anopheles gambiae sensu lato (s.l.)
from togo.
methods: two 5 days old females an. gambiae s.l., originating three
localities (baguida, kovié, kolokopé) subjected insecticide-impregnated
papers 3 years (2012, 2013, 2016) follows: organochlorides (4% ddt),
pyrethroids (0.05% deltamethrin, 0.75% permethrin, 0.05% lambdacyhalothrin),
carbamates (0.4% bendiocarb 0.1% propoxur), organophosphates (5%
malathion, 0.4% chlorpyrifos methyl, 1% fenitrothion) following standard 
protocol. dead surviving mosquitoes stored separately eppendorf tubes
containing silica gel dna extraction, species identification, kdr and
ace-1 genotyping.
results: knockdown times (kdt50 kdt95) high an. gambiae s.l. the
lowest kdts recorded baguida 2013 deltamethrin (kdt50 = 24.7, ci
[22.4-27.12] kdt95 = 90.78, ci [76.35-113.49]). kdts recorded ddt
and instances permethrin. general, an. gambiae s.l. resistant 
to four classes insecticides survey periods regardless 
of locality year, except chlorpyrifos methyl. instances,
mosquitoes fully susceptible fenitrothion (kolokopé: 100% kovié:
98.05%, ci [95.82-100.26]) malathion (100% kolokopé kovié) in
2013, malathion (kolokopé; 100%) 2016. anopheles coluzzii, an.
gambiae anopheles arabiensis three sibling species identified the
three localities hybrids baguida (2013), kovié (2012 2016), 
respectively. anopheles gambiae relatively dominant (61.6%). kdr 1014f
allele frequency was > 0.9 cases, except kolokopé (f
(1014f) = 0.63, ci [0.55-0.71]) 2013. kdr 1014s allele frequency below
0.02. highest ace-1 frequencies identified an. gambiae baguida
(2012: 0.52, ci [0.34-0.69] 2013: 0.66, ci [0.46-0.86]).
conclusion: resistance status worrying togo considered in
future malaria vector resistance management programmes decision-makers.
