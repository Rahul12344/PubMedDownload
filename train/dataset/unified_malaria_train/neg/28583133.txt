background: anopheles sinensis dominant natural vector plasmodium vivax
in china, taiwan, japan, korea. recent genome sequencing an. sinensis
provides important insights genomic basis vectorial capacity.
however, lack physical genome map chromosome assignment and
orientation sequencing scaffolds hinders comparative analyses other
genomes infer evolutionary changes relevant vector capacity.
results: here, physical genome map an. sinensis constructed by
assigning 52 scaffolds onto chromosomes using fluorescence situ
hybridization (fish). chromosome-based genome assembly composes
approximately 36% total an. sinensis genome. comparisons 3955
orthologous genes an. sinensis anopheles gambiae identified 361
conserved synteny blocks 267 inversions fixed two lineages. the
rate gene order reshuffling x chromosome approximately 3.2 times
higher autosomes.
conclusions: physical map facilitate detailed genomic analysis an.
sinensis contribute understanding patterns mechanisms of
large-scale genome rearrangements anopheline mosquitoes.
