background: pfmdr1 variants molecular marker responsible
for antibiotics resistance plasmodium falciparum, parasitic carrier for
malaria disease. novel strategy treat malaria disease disrupting
parasite lactate dehydrogenase (pldh), crucial enzyme plasmodium survival
during erythrocytic stages. research aimed investigate and
characterize pfmdr1 pldh genes p. falciparum isolated nusa
tenggara indonesia.
methods: genomic dna p.falciparum isolated malaria patients nusa
tenggara indonesia. pfmdr1 amplified using nested pcr genotyped using
restriction fragment length polymorphism (rflp). pldh amplified, sequenced,
and analyzed using ncbi public domain databases alignment using clustal w
ver. 1.83.
results: genotyping pfmdr1 revealed sequence diversity extremely 
high among isolates. however, sequence analysis pldh indicated open
reading frame 316 amino acids gene showing 100% homology p.
falciparum 3d7 reference pldh (genebank: xm_001349953.1).
conclusion: first report confirms heterologous pfmdr1
and homologous sequences p.falciparum pldh isolated nusa tenggara
islands indonesia, indicating chloroquine could used
effectively antimalarial target region pldh-targeted
antimalarial compound would higher chance successful using
chloroquine curbing malaria worldwide.
