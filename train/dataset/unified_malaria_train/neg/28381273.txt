background: malaria hotspot southeastern region mauritania, near 
malian border, may hamper malaria control strategies. objectives to
estimate prevalence genetic polymorphisms associated drug resistance 
in plasmodium falciparum isolates establish baseline data.
methods: study conducted two malaria-endemic areas hodh elgharbi,
situated malian-mauritanian border area. blood samples collected from
symptomatic patients. single nucleotide polymorphisms pfcrt, pfmdr1, pfdhfr,
and pfdhps genotyped using pcr-restriction fragment length polymorphism, dna
sequencing primer extension. pfmdr1 gene copy number determined by
real-time pcr.
results: 280 p. falciparum-infected patients, 193 (68.9%) carried pfcrt
76t mutant allele. pfmdr1 86y 184f mutations found 61 (23.1%) 
264 isolates 167 (67.6%) 247 samples successfully genotyped,
respectively. pfmdr1 mutant alleles 1034c, 1042d 1246y rarely observed. 
of 102 p. falciparum isolates analysed, ten (9.8%) one copy of
pfmdr1 gene. prevalence isolates harbouring least triple mutant pfdhfr 
51i, 59r, 108 n/t 42% (112/268), 42 (37.5%) additional pfdhps
437g mutation. pfdhps 540e mutation observed four isolates (1.5%),
including three associated pfdhfr triple mutant. two quintuple mutants 
(pfdhfr-51i-59r-108n pfdhps-437g-540e) observed.
conclusions: observed mutations pfdhfr, pfdhps, pfmdr1, pfcrt may
jeopardize future seasonal malaria chemoprevention based on
amodiaquine-sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine, intermittent preventive treatment for
pregnant women using sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine, and treatment with
artesunate-amodiaquine. complementary studies carried document
the distribution, origin circulation p. falciparum populations this
region widely country assess risk spread of
resistance.
