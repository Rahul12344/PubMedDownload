background: genetic variation plasmodium falciparum histidine-rich
protein 2 (pfhrp2) gene may compromise use pfhrp2-based rapid
diagnostic tests (rdts) diagnosis malaria assessed p.
falciparum isolates yemen.
methods: study conducted hodeidah al-mahwit governorates, yemen. 
a total 622 individuals fever examined malaria carestart
malaria hrp2-rdt giemsa-stained thin thick blood films. pfhrp2 gene
was amplified sequenced 180 isolates, subjected amino acid repeat
types analysis.
results: total 188 (30.2%) participants found positive p.
falciparum rdt. overall, 12 different amino acid repeat types were
identified yemeni isolates. six repeat types detected isolates
(100%) namely types 1, 2, 6, 7, 10 12 types 9 11 detected 
in isolates. moreover, sensitivity specificity used
pfhrp2-based rdts high (90.5% 96.1%, respectively).
conclusion: present study provides data genetic variation within the
pfhrp2 gene, potential impact pfhrp2-based rdts commonly used in
yemen. carestart malaria hrp2-based rdt showed high sensitivity specificity
in endemic areas yemen.
