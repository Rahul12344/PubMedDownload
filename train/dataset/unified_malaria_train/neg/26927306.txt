background: bacterial bloodstream infection (bbsi) one leading causes
of death critically ill patients accurate diagnosis therefore crucial. 
we report 16s metagenomics approach diagnosing understanding bbsi.
methodology/principal findings: proof-of-concept delivered 75 children
(median age 15 months) severe febrile illness burkina faso. standard
blood culture malaria testing conducted time hospital
admission. 16s metagenomics testing done retrospectively duplicate 
the blood patients. total dna extracted blood v3-v4
regions bacterial 16s rrna genes amplified pcr deep sequenced 
on illumina miseq sequencer. paired reads curated, taxonomically labeled,
and filtered. blood culture diagnosed bbsi 12 patients, number
increased 22 patients combining blood culture 16s metagenomics
results. addition superior sensitivity compared standard blood culture, 
16s metagenomics revealed important novel insights nature bbsi.
patients acute malaria recovering malaria 7-fold higher risk
of presenting polymicrobial bloodstream infections compared patients no
recent malaria diagnosis (p-value = 0.046). malaria known affect epithelial
gut function may thus facilitate bacterial translocation intestinal 
lumen blood. importantly, patients polymicrobial blood
infections showed 9-fold higher risk factor surviving febrile
illness (p-value = 0.030).
conclusions/significance: data demonstrate 16s metagenomics a
powerful approach diagnosis understanding bbsi. this
proof-of-concept study also showed appropriate control samples crucial
to detect background signals due environmental contamination.
