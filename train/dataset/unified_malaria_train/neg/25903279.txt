background: long non-coding rnas (lncrnas) defined mrna-like
transcripts longer 200 nucleotides lack significant protein-coding
potential, many constitute scaffolds ribonucleoprotein complexes 
with critical roles epigenetic regulation. various lncrnas been
implicated modulation chromatin structure, transcriptional and
post-transcriptional gene regulation, regulation genomic stability in
mammals, caenorhabditis elegans, drosophila melanogaster. purpose this
study identify lncrna landscape malaria vector an. gambiae and
assess evolutionary conservation lncrnas secondary structures
across anopheles genus.
results: using deep rna sequencing multiple anopheles gambiae life stages, 
have identified 2,949 lncrnas 300 previously unannotated putative
protein-coding genes. lncrnas exhibit differential expression profiles across
life stages adult genders. find across genus anopheles, lncrnas
display much lower sequence conservation protein-coding genes. additionally,
we find lncrna secondary structure highly conserved within gambiae
complex, diverges rapidly across rest genus anopheles.
conclusions: study offers one first lncrna secondary structure
analyses vector insects. description lncrnas an. gambiae offers 
most comprehensive genome-wide insights date lncrnas vector
mosquito, defines set potential targets development of
vector-based interventions may curb human malaria burden in
disease-endemic countries.
