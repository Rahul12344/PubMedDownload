plasmodium knowlesi hiv co-infection german traveller thailand.

ehrhardt j, trein a, kremsner p, frank m.

a case plasmodium knowlesi hiv co-infection reported german
traveller returning thailand. 54 year-old patient presented the
institute tropical medicine tübingen 11-day history daily fever
and chills. initial microscopic evaluation giemsa-stained thin blood smears
was suggestive mixed infection plasmodium falciparum plasmodium
malariae. however, pcr amplification small subunit ribosomal rna gene revealed
a p. knowlesi infection. parasitaemia 473 parasites/μl platelet count
was within normal range. oral treatment malarone® initiated and
resulted fast recovery without complications. part routine
screening patient also underwent hiv testing found hiv positive
with cd4 cell count 115/μl viral load 34,799 copies/ml. follow-up
measurement viral load seven days first quantification revealed 
an increase 102,000 copies/ml. three months first quantification the
viral load dropped 10,000 copies/ml without initiation of
antiretroviral treatment. suggests possibility p. knowlesi
malaria-induced temporary elevation viral load similar reported p.
falciparum hiv co-infection.
