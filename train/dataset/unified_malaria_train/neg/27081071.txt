a commonly used antimalarial medication, atovaquone, apparently unable to
spread. atovaquone pressure selects parasites mutations cytochrome b, a
respiratory protein low essential activity mammalian blood phase 
of parasite life cycle. resistance mutations rescue parasites drug
but later prove lethal mosquito phase, parasites require full
respiration. unable respire efficiently, resistant parasites fail complete 
mosquito development, arresting life cycle. cytochrome b encoded
by maternally inherited parasite mitochondrion, even outcrossing with
wild-type strains cannot facilitate spread resistance. lack transmission
suggests resistance unable spread field, greatly enhancing
the utility atovaquone malaria control.
