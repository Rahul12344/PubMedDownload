background: malaria vector-borne parasitic disease prevalent in
many developing countries. recently, found plasmodium knowlesi, 
a simian malaria parasite life-threatening humans. long-tailed
macaques, widely distributed malaysia, natural hosts for
simian malaria, including p. knowlesi. aim present study to
determine prevalence simian malaria parasites long-tailed macaques in
the district hulu selangor, selangor, malaysia.
methods: total 70 blood samples collected macaca fascicularis
dwelling forest hulu selangor department wildlife and
national parks peninsular malaysia, kuala lumpur, malaysia. dna extracted
using purelinkâ„¢ genomic dna kits. conventional nested pcr used detect
the genus species plasmodium parasites respectively. addition,
phylogenetic analysis carried confirm species plasmodium
parasites.
results: thirty-five (50 %) 70 samples positive plasmodium using 
genus-specific primers. positive samples subjected nested pcr 
targeting 18s ribosomal rna genes detect five simian malaria
parasites: namely, p. knowlesi, plasmodium inui, plasmodium cynomolgi, plasmodium
fieldi, plasmodium coatneyi. five species simian malaria parasites
were detected. these, p. inui predominant (65.7 %), followed p.
knowlesi (60 %), p. cynomolgi (51.4 %) p. coatneyi (45.7 %) p. fieldi (2.9
%). total nine macaques mono-infection p. knowlesi (four), p.
cynomolgi (two), p. coatneyi (two) p. fieldi (one). eleven macaques
had dual infections 12 triple infections. three macaques infected 
with four species plasmodium. molecular phylogenetic analysis confirmed
the five species plasmodium parasites.
conclusion: study provided evidence elucidate presence of
transmission malaria parasites among local macaques hulu selangor.
since malaria zoonosis, important determine new control
strategies control malaria.
