background: sri lanka achieved 'malaria-free' status phase 
of prevention re-introduction malaria. imported malaria remains challenge
to resurgence disease. diagnostic challenges encountered rapid
response initiated manage plasmodium infection, later confirmed as
plasmodium knowlesi, first reported case sri lanka, discussed.
case presentation: army officer returned malaysia october 2016 was
found positive plasmodium microscopy rapid diagnostic test 
(rdt) anti malaria campaign sri lanka (amc) third visit a
health care provider. microscopy findings suspicious p. knowlesi
infection smears showed parasite stages similar plasmodium
malariae plasmodium falciparum. nested pcr amc confirmed plasmodium genus,
but species. absence species confirmation, patient was
treated case p. falciparum. presence p. knowlesi later
confirmed semi-nested pcr assay performed environmental health
institute, national environmental agency singapore. parasite strain was
also characterized sequencing circumsporozoite gene. extensive case
investigation including parasitological entomological surveillance was
carried out.
conclusions: plasmodium knowlesi suspected patients returning from
countries south asian region parasite prevalent when
blood smear results inconclusive.
