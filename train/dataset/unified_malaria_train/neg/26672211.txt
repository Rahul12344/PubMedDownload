[analysis msp1 allelotypes imported cases plasmodium falciparum malaria 
from africa].

[article chinese]

zhou sm, yang y, wu k, che z, xu mx, jia xs.

objective: understand allelotype characteristics merozoite surface 
protein 1 (msp1) imported plasmodium falciparum.
methods: blood samples collected p. falciparum-infected patients
returning african malaria endemic areas. nested pcr used amplify gene
fragments msp1 coding block 2 block 3 motifs msp1 p. falciparum 
by using msp1-specific primers. allelotype mspi analyzed.
results: msp1 allelotype detected 117 135 blood samples, a
detection rate 86.7%. 117 cases successful pcr amplification, 
detection rates mad20, k1, ro33, mad20+k1, mad20+ro33, k1+ro33 and
mad20+k1+r033 6.0%(7/117), 36.8%(43/117), 20.5%(24/117), 6.8% (8/117), 3.4% 
(4/117), 17.1% (20/117) 9.4% (11/117), respectively, wherein mixed
infection accounted 36.8%(43/135). mean multiplicity infection(moi) of
msp1 allelotype 1.46. significant difference proportion
of patients major severity illness among mad20, k1 ro33
genotypes. proportions patients major severity illness were
25.7%(19/74) 32.6%(14/43) 74 cases singular infection 43 cases of
mixed infection, respectively. two infection types patients 241 ± 176 
days 285 ± 216 days stay abroad, significant difference between
them.
conclusion: three genotypes msp1 four types combination exist
in imported cases p. falciparum malaria africa. k1 ro33 the
dominant genotves.
