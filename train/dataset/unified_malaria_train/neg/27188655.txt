background: louse-borne relapsing fever (lbrf) neglected disease has
been restricted east africa many decades. several cases refugees from
the horn africa recently diagnosed four european countries.
case presentation: report four additional cases lbrf asylum seekers from
somalia eritrea presented fever shortly arriving in
switzerland seven-month period. multiple spirochetes visualized 
stained blood films identified borrelia recurrentis 16s rrna
gene sequencing. patients recovered antibiotic treatment with
ceftriaxone and/or doxycycline. concurrent infections (malaria tuberculosis) 
were diagnosed half patients. possible modes transmission and
preventive measures discussed.
conclusions: reported cases highlight ongoing transmission lbrf in
migrants east africa. diagnosis lbrf cases prevention of
autochthonous transmission asylum seeker camps important steps the
near future.
