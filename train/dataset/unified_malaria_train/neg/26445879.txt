background: haemoglobin (hbs) gene known confer strongest
advantage malaria morbidity mortality. multiple hbs effects been
described resulting protection parasitaemia reduction severe
malaria risk. study aimed explore hbs protection severe malaria
and plasmodium falciparum parasitaemia angolan children exhibiting different
severe malaria syndromes.
methods: case-control study designed 430 malaria cases (n = 288 severe
malaria n = 142 uncomplicated malaria) 319 uninfected controls, attending
a central paediatric hospital luanda. severe malaria syndromes cerebral
malaria (n = 130), severe malaria anaemia (n = 30) hyperparasitaemia (n =
128). quantitative trait locus analysis carried study hbs association 
to parasite densities.
results: previously reported hbs protection severe malaria confirmed 
in case-control analysis (p = 2 × 10(-13)) corroborated transmission
disequilibrium test (p = 4 × 10(-3)). high parasite density protection conferred 
by hbs detectable within severe malaria patients (p = 0.04). stratifying
severe malaria patients according parasite densities, found hbs was
highly associated hyperparasitaemia protection (p = 1.9 × 10(-9)) 
protect non-hyperparasitaemic children severe malaria complications,
namely cerebral malaria severe malaria anaemia. many studies shown 
hbs protects severe malaria controls parasite densities analysis
further suggests hbs protection severe malaria syndromes a
large extent correlated control parasitaemia levels.
conclusions: study supports hypothesis hbs confers resistance to
hyperparasitaemia patients exhibiting severe malaria syndromes highlights 
that parasitaemia taken account evaluating hbs protection 
severe malaria.
