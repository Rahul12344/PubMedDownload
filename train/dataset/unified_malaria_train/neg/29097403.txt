the identification genomic rearrangements high sensitivity and
specificity using massively parallel sequencing remains major challenge,
particularly precision medicine cancer research. here, describe new
method detecting rearrangements, gridss (genome rearrangement identification 
software suite). gridss multithreaded structural variant (sv) caller that
performs efficient genome-wide break-end assembly prior variant calling using 
a novel positional de bruijn graph-based assembler. combining assembly, split 
read, read pair evidence using probabilistic scoring, gridss achieves high 
sensitivity specificity simulated, cell line, patient tumor data,
recently winning sv subchallenge #5 icgc-tcga dream8.5 somatic mutation
calling challenge. human cell line data, gridss halves false discovery
rate compared recent methods matching exceeding their
sensitivity. gridss identifies nontemplate sequence insertions, microhomologies, 
and large imperfect homologies, estimates quality score breakpoint,
stratifies calls high low confidence, supports multisample analysis.
