malaria parasites complete intra-erythrocytic developmental cycle (idc) 
multiples 24 h suggesting circadian basis, mechanism controlling
this periodicity unknown. combining vivo vitro approaches utilizing 
rodent human malaria parasites, reveal that: (i) 57% plasmodium
chabaudi genes exhibit daily rhythms transcription; (ii) 58% genes
lose transcriptional rhythmicity idc out-of-synchrony host
rhythms; (iii) 6% plasmodium falciparum genes show 24 h rhythms expression 
under free-running conditions; (iv) serpentine receptor 10 (sr10) 24 h
transcriptional rhythm disrupting rodent malaria parasites shortens the
idc 2-3 h; (v) multiple processes including dna replication, ubiquitin
and proteasome pathways, affected loss coordination host rhythms
and disruption sr10. results reveal malaria parasites least
partly responsible scheduling idc coordinating development with
host daily rhythms.
