background: insecticide susceptibility study carried anopheles
gambiae sensu lato vectors 11 districts ghana october 2012 and
january 2013.
methods: an. gambiae s.l. larvae collected, bred standard conditions, 
and 3-5 d postemerged females used bioassay. 22 25 female
an. gambiae s.l. fed 10% sugar used testing. exposure 1 h
(2 h fenitrothion). gambiae knocked recorded every
10 min mortalities recorded 24 h posttest. eleven insecticides four
chemical classes used: organochlorines, organophosphates, carbamates, and
pyrethroids. subsamples gambiae analyzed polymerase chain reaction 
for species knockdown resistance (kdr) allele determination.
results: malathion effective killing an. gambiae seven districts,
fenitrothion three districts, propoxur one district. the
organophosphate carbamate insecticides effective killing an. gambiae 
compared pyrethroids organochlorines. limited samples analyzed, an.
gambiae sensu stricto (39/110), an. coluzzii (66/110), an. arabiensis (5/110)
were identified. kdr (11/110) susceptible mosquitoes detected.
homozygous kdrrr (65/110) heterozygous kdrrs (8/110) genotypes were
identified.
conclusions: organophosphate insecticide considered appropriate indoor 
residual spraying (irs) 11 districts earmarked irs program in
ghana.
