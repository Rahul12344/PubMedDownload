background: avian malaria occurs almost worldwide caused haemosporida
parasites (plasmodium, haemoproteus leucocytozoon). vectors as
mosquitoes, hippoboscid flies biting midges required transmission 
of parasites. studies avian malaria parasites on
madagascar none suitable vectors.
methods: identify vectors avian plasmodium parasites madagascar, we
examined head, thorax abdomen 418 mosquitoes least 18 species
using nested pcr method amplify 524 bp fragment haemosporidian
mitochondrial cytochrome b gene. sequences obtained compared a
large dataset haemosporidian sequences detected 45 different bird species
(n = 686) area maromizaha rainforest.
results: twenty-one mosquitoes tested positive avian malaria parasites.
haemoproteus dna found nine mosquitoes (2.15%) plasmodium dna was
found 12 mosquitoes (2.87%). seven distinct lineages identified among the
plasmodium dna samples. lineages also found examined bird
samples: plasmodium sp. wa46 (eu810628.1) madagascar bulbul, plasmodium
sp. mosquito 132 (ab308050.1) 15 bird species belonging eight families,
plasmodium sp. pv12 (gq150194.1) eleven bird species belonging eight
families plasmodium sp. p31 (dq839060.1) found three weaver bird
species.
conclusion: study provides first insight avian malaria transmission
in maromizaha rainforest eastern madagascar. five haemoproteus lineages
and seven plasmodium lineages detected examined mosquitoes. complete 
life-cycles specialist lineages wa46 p31 generalist
lineages mosquito132 pv12 plasmodium proposed. addition, have
identified first time anopheles mascarensis uranotaenia spp. as
vectors avian malaria offer first description vector mosquitoes
