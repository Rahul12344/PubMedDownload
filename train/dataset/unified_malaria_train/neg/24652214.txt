introduction: rapid diagnostic tests (rdt) postulated way to
ensure access malaria diagnosis remote areas. despite widespread use,
there field studies evaluate accuracy sd bioline malaria
antigen pf/pv colombia rdt.
objective: evaluate diagnostic accuracy sd bioline malaria antigen 
pf/pv® rdt two departments endemic malaria, comparing diagnosis thick
film corrected pcr.
materials methods: retrospective study carried evaluate
sensitivity, specificity, positive predictive value (ppv), negative predictive
value (npv), concordance sensitivity limits according parasitemia ranges
for sd bioline malaria antigen pf/pv ® test cordoba choco. results
were compared microscopy corrected pcr.
results: total 383 samples processed, 121 positive (75 p. vivax ,
42 p. falciparum 4 mixed infection) 262 negative samples. p.
vivax: sensitivity 92.0% (95% ci: 83.6-96.3), specificity 98.7% ( 95% ci:
96.7-99.5), ppv 94.5% (95% ci: 86.7-97.9), npv 98.1% (95% ci: 95.8-99.1), cohen's
kappa coefficient 0.90 (0.80-1.00). p. falciparum: sensitivity 88.1% (95% ci:
75.0-94.8), specificity 97.9% (95% ci: 95.8-99.0), ppv 84.1% (95% ci: 70.6-92.1),
npv 98.5% (95% ic: 96.6-99.4), cohen's kappa coefficient 0.80 (95% ci:
0.70-0.90).
conclusions: test performed well, better p. vivax compared p.
falciparum. still difficulties rdt detect low parasitemias. the
non amplification pfhrp2 pfhrp3 genes two samples diagnosed mixed
infection, suggest possible deletion two genes together.
