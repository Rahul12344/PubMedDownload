zanthoxylum zanthoxyloides west african forest tree used example
against malaria sickle cell anemia burkina faso. goal study
was analyze genetic morphological diversity species within wild
populations burkina faso, potentially threat due the
uncontrolled harvesting roots. seventy-two trees three different
sites southwestern burkina faso analyzed. tree characterized 
12 traits specifying period flowering maturity well as
morphological characteristics stem, leaves, seeds. molecular
analysis performed using two plastid dna regions (psba-trnh trnl-trnf)
and two nuclear regions (gbssi its) identify genetic diversity 
species development management plan ex situ reproduction and
in situ conservation. found variability morphological traits correlating
with geographic distance study sites. molecular analysis, in
contrast, revealed hardly genetic variability among tested trees no
population structure. whether differences morphological traits caused 
by different environmental conditions genetic variability genes linked
to morphological traits needs testing. apparent lack genetic
differentiation suggests germplasm throughout study region suitable
for planting conservation actions. efficient conservation management should
involve local communities, especially interested traditional medicine.
