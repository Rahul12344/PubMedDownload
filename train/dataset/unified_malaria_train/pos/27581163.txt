background: plasmodium vivax causative agent human malaria large
geographic distribution, 35 million cases annually. brazil, the
most prevalent species, responsible around 70 % malaria cases.
methods: cross-sectional study performed manaus (amazonas, brazil),
including 36 adult patients primary malaria, 19 recurrent malaria, 
20 endemic controls. ex vivo phenotypic features circulating leukocyte
subsets (cd4(+) t-cells, cd8(+) t-cells, nk, nkt, b, b1 treg cells) well
as plasmatic cytokine profile (il-2, il-4, il-6, il-10, tnf ifn-γ) were
assessed, aiming establishing patterns immune response characteristic of
primary malaria vs recurrent malaria compared endemic controls.
results: proportion subjects high levels wbc reduced in
malaria patients compared endemic control. monocytes diminished
particularly patients primary malaria. proportion subjects with
high levels lymphocyte subsets decreased malaria groups,
regardless clinical status. decreased proportion subjects high
levels cd4(+) cd8(+) t-cells found especially group patients
with recurrent malaria. data analysis indicated significant increase the
proportion subjects high plasmatic cytokine levels malaria
groups, characterizing typical cytokine storm. recurrent malaria patients
displayed highest plasmatic il-10 levels, correlated directly the
cd4(+)/cd8(+) t-cells ratio number malaria episodes.
conclusion: findings confirm infection p. vivax causes a
decrease peripheral blood lymphocyte subsets, intensified the
cases "recurrent malaria". unbalanced cd4(+)/cd8(+) t-cells ratio, well
as increased il-10 levels correlated number recurrent malaria
episodes. results suggest gradual remodelling immune
response dependent repeated exposure parasite, involves 
strict control immune response mediated cd4(+)/cd8(+) t-cell
unbalance exacerbated il-10 secretion.
