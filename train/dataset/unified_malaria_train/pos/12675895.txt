infection babesia bovis diagnosed 2-day-old female calf apparently 
transmitted utero. calf born second calving cross-bred
beef cow permanently pasture. diagnosis based upon identification b.
bovis peripheral blood smears clinical signs included fever,
jaundice, pale mucous membranes convulsions. anaemia, leucocytosis,
thrombocytopenia lymphocytosis noted febrile acute stage the
disease. blood smears revealed evidence regeneration toxic neutrophils 
with left shift, severe spherocytosis high degree basophilic stippling. 
elevated concentration aspartate aminotransferase, lactate dehydrogenase, 
creatine kinase also noted, probably result haemolysis,
dehydration muscle damage recumbancy. elevated total bilirubin
concentration following haemolysis resulted jaundice. neurological
symptoms observed probably caused sludging parasitized erythrocytes in
the brain capillaries. calf recovered following treatment diminazene
aceturate recovery followed clinically, haematologically and
biochemically.
