but reliably replicated. collected data 11,890 cases of
severe malaria due plasmodium falciparum 17,441 controls 12 locations
in africa, asia oceania. tested 55 snps 27 loci previously reported 
associate severe malaria. evidence association p < 1 ×
10(-4) hbb, abo, atp2b4, g6pd cd40lg loci, previously reported
associations 22 loci replicate multicenter analysis. 
large sample size made possible identify authentic genetic effects are
heterogeneous across populations phenotypes, striking example the
main african form g6pd deficiency, reduced risk cerebral malaria 
but increased risk severe malarial anemia. finding g6pd
deficiency opposing effects different fatal complications p. falciparum
infection indicates evolutionary origins common human genetic
disorder complex previously supposed.
