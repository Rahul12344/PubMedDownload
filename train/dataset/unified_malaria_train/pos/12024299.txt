a 10-year-old spayed female mixed-breed dog presented purdue
university veterinary teaching hospital (puvth) complaints persistent
anemia occasional exacerbations, anorexia, lethargy. dog been
presented referring veterinarian 2 months prior multiple bite wounds 
received fight 3 pit bull terriers. dog discharged after
the wounds cleaned surgically closed. upon admission puvth, blood
was collected complete blood count biochemical analysis. microscopic
examination peripheral blood smears revealed intraerythrocytic protozoal
parasites consistent babesia gibsoni. molecular analysis confirmed 
organism b. gibsoni 18s ribosomal rna sequence identical 
that b. gibsoni isolates oklahoma, north carolina, okinawa,
japan. hematologic changes included moderately severe, regenerative, macrocytic, 
normochromic anemia, poikilocytosis, polychromasia, anisocytosis, a
marked increase nucleated rbcs. biochemical changes included increased serum
alanine aminotransferase, alkaline phosphatase, gamma-glutamyltransferase
activities. dog treated imidocarb, despite initial clinical
improvement, dog died 2 weeks first dose. necropsy not
performed. infection dog first reported case b. gibsoni
infection indiana. widespread geographical distribution 
organism, veterinarians veterinary clinical pathologists throughout the
united states carefully examine romanowsky-stained blood smears from
patients acute hemolytic anemia small intraerythrocytic babesial
parasites.
