Primary human immunodeficiency virus infection presenting as acute pancreatitis.

Tyner R(1), Turett G.

Author information: 
(1)Section of Infectious Diseases, Department of Medicine, St. Vincent's Catholic
Medical Center, New York, NY 10011, USA.

Primary human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection can present in a variety of 
ways. It is important to recognize acute HIV infection, for personal and public
health reasons. We present an unusual case of primary HIV infection manifesting
as acute pancreatitis.
