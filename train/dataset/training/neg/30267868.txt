For chronic hepatitis B patients, hepatitis B e antigen (HBeAg) seroclearance
signals a transition from an immunologically active phase to an inactive carrier 
state with a reduction in hepatitis B virus (HBV) DNA levels and a reduced risk
of hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC).1 Predictors of HBeAg seroclearance include
lower HBV DNA levels, viral genotype, the precore mutation, and higher serum
alanine aminotransferase (ALT) levels.2.
