Human immunodeficiency virus in breast milk.

Kennedy SB 4th(1).

Author information: 
(1)School of Public Health, Allegheny University of the Health Sciences,
Philadelphia, PA 19102, USA. kennedys@allegheny.edu

As the acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS) epidemic spreads to the
pediatric population, a recommendation is made for more research on
mother-to-infant human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) transmission, in light of
current policies, and the scientific community is challenged to re-evaluate its
attitude to the pathogenesis of HIV transmission by breast milk.
