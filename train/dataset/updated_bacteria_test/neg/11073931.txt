
1. J Bacteriol. 2000 Dec;182(23):6827-30.

Comparison of the UDP-N-acetylmuramate:L-alanine ligase enzymes from
Mycobacterium tuberculosis and Mycobacterium leprae.

Mahapatra S(1), Crick DC, Brennan PJ.

Author information: 
(1)Department of Microbiology, Colorado State University, Fort Collins, Colorado 
80523-1677, USA.

In the peptidoglycan of Mycobacterium leprae, L-alanine of the side chain is
replaced by glycine. When expressed in Escherichia coli, MurC
(UDP-N-acetyl-muramate:L-alanine ligase) of M. leprae showed K(m) and V(max) for 
L-alanine and glycine similar to those of Mycobacterium tuberculosis MurC,
suggesting that another explanation should be sought for the presence of glycine.
